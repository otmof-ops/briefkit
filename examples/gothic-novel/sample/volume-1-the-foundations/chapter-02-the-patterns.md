# Chapter 2: The Patterns

---

**Bold prefix paragraphs.** A chapter that opens with `**Bold.**` (a sub-heading-like prefix on the first paragraph) renders the prefix bold and skips the drop cap on that chapter — the cap glyph would tangle with the bold HTML otherwise.

The body continues normally after the bold prefix, with no drop cap.

---

## Sub-section header

Lines that start with `## ` become sub-section headings, set in Fraktur display type centered on the page. Useful for prophet sections, named sub-arcs, or any in-chapter division you want to mark with hierarchical weight.

After a sub-header, body resumes normally:

The wind that hovered over the waters at creation. The wind that blew the locusts into Egypt. The wind that parted the Red Sea.

---

## Another sub-section

Multiple sub-sections within one chapter are fine. Each one resets the running paragraph flow.

You can mix bold prefixes and sub-headers within the same chapter as needed. The parser handles them independently.
