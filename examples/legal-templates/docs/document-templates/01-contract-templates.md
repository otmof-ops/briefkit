## Overview

This document provides four foundational contract templates used across commercial and personal legal practice: the non-disclosure agreement, the services agreement, the employment contract, and the residential lease. Each template includes the complete text with bracketed variables, clause-by-clause annotation, jurisdiction-specific variation notes, and common pitfalls.

All templates assume arm's-length transactions between parties of roughly equal bargaining power. Where consumer protection legislation imposes additional requirements (such as unfair contract terms provisions under the Australian Consumer Law or the Consumer Rights Act 2015 (UK)), those requirements are noted but may necessitate further adaptation.

## Template 1: Non-Disclosure Agreement (Mutual)

### Purpose

A non-disclosure agreement (NDA), also called a confidentiality agreement or confidential disclosure agreement (CDA), creates legally binding obligations to protect confidential information shared between parties. It is typically used before business negotiations, potential partnerships, due diligence processes, or any engagement where proprietary information will be exchanged.

### When to Use

- Pre-transaction due diligence (mergers, acquisitions, joint ventures)
- Discussions with potential suppliers, contractors, or business partners
- Sharing trade secrets, business plans, financial data, or technical information
- Employee access to sensitive business information (though employment contracts often include confidentiality clauses)

### Template Text

---

**MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT**

**Date:** [DATE OF AGREEMENT]

**Parties:**

1. [PARTY A FULL LEGAL NAME] ([ACN/ABN/COMPANY NUMBER]) of [PARTY A REGISTERED ADDRESS] (**"Party A"**)
2. [PARTY B FULL LEGAL NAME] ([ACN/ABN/COMPANY NUMBER]) of [PARTY B REGISTERED ADDRESS] (**"Party B"**)

(each a **"Party"** and together the **"Parties"**)

**Background**

A. The Parties are considering [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF PURPOSE, e.g., "a potential commercial transaction involving the supply of technology services"] (the **"Purpose"**).

B. In connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose Confidential Information to the other Party.

C. The Parties wish to protect the confidentiality of such information on the terms set out in this Agreement.

**Agreed Terms**

**1. Definitions and Interpretation**

1.1 In this Agreement:

**"Confidential Information"** means all information disclosed by a Party (the **"Disclosing Party"**) to the other Party (the **"Receiving Party"**) in connection with the Purpose, whether disclosed orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, and whether or not marked as confidential, including:

(a) trade secrets, know-how, inventions, processes, techniques, and algorithms;

(b) business plans, financial information, customer lists, supplier information, and pricing;

(c) technical data, designs, specifications, and software;

(d) any copies, summaries, notes, or analyses derived from the above; and

(e) the existence and terms of this Agreement and the fact that discussions regarding the Purpose are taking place,

but excludes information that:

(f) is or becomes publicly available other than through breach of this Agreement;

(g) was lawfully in the Receiving Party's possession before disclosure, as evidenced by written records;

(h) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to the Confidential Information; or

(i) is disclosed to the Receiving Party by a third party who is not bound by any obligation of confidentiality in respect of that information.

**"Permitted Purpose"** means [SPECIFIC PERMITTED USE, e.g., "evaluating and negotiating the potential transaction described in the Background"].

**"Representatives"** means, in relation to a Party, its directors, officers, employees, agents, advisors (including legal, financial, and technical advisors), and contractors who need access to the Confidential Information for the Permitted Purpose.

1.2 A reference to a statute includes any amendment, re-enactment, or replacement of that statute and any subordinate legislation made under it.

**2. Confidentiality Obligations**

2.1 The Receiving Party must:

(a) keep the Confidential Information strictly confidential;

(b) not disclose the Confidential Information to any person except its Representatives, and only to the extent necessary for the Permitted Purpose;

(c) use the Confidential Information solely for the Permitted Purpose;

(d) take all reasonable steps to protect the Confidential Information from unauthorized disclosure, including measures at least as protective as those the Receiving Party uses for its own confidential information of similar sensitivity; and

(e) ensure that each Representative to whom Confidential Information is disclosed is made aware of and complies with the obligations in this Agreement.

2.2 The Receiving Party is responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

**3. Compelled Disclosure**

3.1 If the Receiving Party is compelled by law, regulation, or valid court order to disclose Confidential Information, the Receiving Party must:

(a) to the extent legally permitted, promptly notify the Disclosing Party before making such disclosure;

(b) cooperate with the Disclosing Party (at the Disclosing Party's cost) in any attempt to limit or prevent the disclosure; and

(c) disclose only the minimum amount of Confidential Information required by law.

**4. Return and Destruction**

4.1 Upon the earlier of: (a) the Disclosing Party's written request; or (b) the termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party must promptly:

(a) return to the Disclosing Party all documents and materials containing Confidential Information; or

(b) destroy all such documents and materials (including electronic copies),

and certify in writing that it has done so. The Receiving Party may retain copies required by law or regulation, or stored on automatic backup systems in the ordinary course of business, provided that such retained copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations in this Agreement.

**5. No Grant of Rights**

5.1 Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any right, title, or interest in the Confidential Information or any intellectual property rights of the Disclosing Party.

5.2 Neither Party is obliged to disclose any information or to enter into any further agreement.

**6. Remedies**

6.1 The Parties acknowledge that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which damages may not be an adequate remedy. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party is entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief in addition to any other remedies available at law.

**7. Term and Survival**

7.1 This Agreement commences on the date first written above and continues for [TERM, e.g., "two (2) years"] unless terminated earlier by either Party giving [NOTICE PERIOD, e.g., "thirty (30) days"] written notice.

7.2 The confidentiality obligations in clause 2 survive termination of this Agreement for a period of [SURVIVAL PERIOD, e.g., "five (5) years"] from the date of termination, or indefinitely in respect of trade secrets.

**8. General**

8.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of [GOVERNING JURISDICTION, e.g., "New South Wales, Australia"] and the Parties submit to the non-exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of that jurisdiction.

8.2 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties in relation to its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and representations.

8.3 **Amendment.** This Agreement may only be amended in writing signed by both Parties.

8.4 **Waiver.** A waiver of any provision of this Agreement must be in writing and does not constitute a waiver of any other provision.

8.5 **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force and effect.

8.6 **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in any number of counterparts, each of which is an original and all of which together constitute one agreement.

**Executed** as an agreement.

[PARTY A FULL LEGAL NAME]

Signature: ________________________
Name: [SIGNATORY NAME]
Title: [SIGNATORY TITLE]
Date: [DATE]

[PARTY B FULL LEGAL NAME]

Signature: ________________________
Name: [SIGNATORY NAME]
Title: [SIGNATORY TITLE]
Date: [DATE]

### Key Clauses Explained

| Clause | Purpose | Risk if Omitted |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Definition of Confidential Information | Defines scope of protection | Disputes about what is covered |
| Exclusions (1.1(f)-(i)) | Prevents overreach; ensures enforceability | May be found unconscionable or unenforceable |
| Compelled Disclosure (3) | Protects party from criminal liability for obeying court orders | Party faces impossible choice between breach of agreement and contempt of court |
| Return/Destruction (4) | Limits ongoing exposure after relationship ends | Information remains accessible indefinitely |
| Remedies (6) | Establishes entitlement to injunctive relief | Must prove entitlement to injunction from scratch |
| Survival (7.2) | Extends protection beyond agreement term | Protection ceases when agreement terminates |

### Jurisdiction Variations

| Issue | AU | US | UK | CA | NZ |
|-------|----|----|----|----|-----|
| Consideration required | No (if deed) | Yes (mutual disclosure usually sufficient) | No (if deed) | Yes | No (if deed) |
| Non-compete in NDA | Enforceable if reasonable | Varies by state; void in CA (Bus & Prof Code s 16600) | Enforceable if reasonable | Enforceable if reasonable | Enforceable if reasonable |
| Injunctive relief | Equitable discretion | Available; some states require bond | Equitable discretion | Equitable discretion | Equitable discretion |
| Electronic execution | Valid under ETA 1999 | Valid under E-SIGN Act / UETA | Valid under ECA 2000 | Valid under provincial e-commerce acts | Valid under ETA 2002 |

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Overbroad definition of confidential information.** Defining "all information shared" as confidential, without meaningful exclusions, may render the agreement unenforceable as an unreasonable restraint of trade.
2. **No end date on confidentiality.** Perpetual confidentiality obligations are difficult to enforce for non-trade-secret information. Define a reasonable survival period.
3. **One-way NDA for mutual disclosure.** If both parties will share information, use a mutual NDA. A one-way agreement leaves the disclosing party unprotected.
4. **Failure to address compelled disclosure.** Without clause 3, a party served with a subpoena faces an impossible conflict between compliance with the NDA and compliance with the law.
5. **No carve-out for automatic backups.** Modern IT systems create automatic backups. Without the carve-out in clause 4.1, the return/destroy obligation may be practically impossible to comply with.

## Template 2: Services Agreement

### Purpose

A services agreement governs the engagement of one party to provide services to another. It addresses the scope of services, payment terms, intellectual property ownership, liability allocation, and termination rights. It applies to independent contractor engagements, consultancy arrangements, and outsourced service delivery.

### When to Use

- Engaging consultants, contractors, or freelancers
- Outsourcing business functions (IT, marketing, accounting, HR)
- Software development or implementation projects
- Professional advisory engagements (where not governed by profession-specific retainer agreements)

### Template Text

---

**SERVICES AGREEMENT**

**Date:** [DATE OF AGREEMENT]

**Parties:**

1. [CLIENT FULL LEGAL NAME] ([ACN/ABN/COMPANY NUMBER]) of [CLIENT REGISTERED ADDRESS] (**"Client"**)
2. [PROVIDER FULL LEGAL NAME] ([ACN/ABN/COMPANY NUMBER]) of [PROVIDER REGISTERED ADDRESS] (**"Provider"**)

**Background**

A. The Client wishes to engage the Provider to perform the Services described in this Agreement.

B. The Provider has represented that it has the necessary skills, qualifications, and resources to perform the Services.

**Agreed Terms**

**1. Services**

1.1 The Provider must perform the services described in **Schedule 1** (the **"Services"**) in accordance with this Agreement.

1.2 The Provider must perform the Services:
(a) with due care, skill, and diligence;
(b) in accordance with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards;
(c) in a timely manner consistent with the milestones set out in Schedule 1; and
(d) using personnel with the qualifications and experience specified in Schedule 1.

1.3 The Provider must comply with all reasonable directions of the Client in relation to the performance of the Services, provided such directions do not fundamentally alter the scope of the Services.

**2. Fees and Payment**

2.1 The Client must pay the Provider the fees set out in **Schedule 2** (the **"Fees"**) in accordance with this clause 2.

2.2 The Provider must submit invoices [INVOICING FREQUENCY, e.g., "monthly in arrears"] in a form reasonably acceptable to the Client, including sufficient detail of the Services performed.

2.3 The Client must pay each correctly rendered invoice within [PAYMENT TERMS, e.g., "thirty (30) days"] of receipt.

2.4 All Fees are exclusive of [GST/VAT/SALES TAX] unless otherwise stated. If [GST/VAT/SALES TAX] is payable on a supply made under this Agreement, the Client must pay the [GST/VAT/SALES TAX] amount in addition to the Fees.

2.5 The Client may dispute an invoice in good faith by notifying the Provider within [DISPUTE PERIOD, e.g., "fourteen (14) days"] of receipt. The undisputed portion of the invoice remains payable in accordance with clause 2.3.

**3. Intellectual Property**

3.1 **Pre-existing IP.** Each Party retains ownership of its intellectual property that existed before this Agreement or that is developed independently of this Agreement (**"Pre-existing IP"**).

3.2 **Project IP.** All intellectual property created by the Provider in the course of performing the Services (**"Project IP"**) [SELECT ONE]:

[OPTION A — Assignment to Client]
vests in and is assigned to the Client upon creation. The Provider must do all things necessary to give effect to this assignment, including executing any documents reasonably required by the Client. The Provider irrevocably and unconditionally consents to any act or omission that would otherwise infringe any moral rights the Provider may have in the Project IP.

[OPTION B — License to Client]
remains the property of the Provider. The Provider grants the Client a perpetual, irrevocable, non-exclusive, royalty-free, worldwide license to use, reproduce, modify, and sublicense the Project IP for the Client's business purposes.

3.3 **License of Pre-existing IP.** To the extent that the Services or Project IP incorporate the Provider's Pre-existing IP, the Provider grants the Client a perpetual, irrevocable, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use such Pre-existing IP to the extent necessary to receive the full benefit of the Services and Project IP.

**4. Confidentiality**

4.1 Each Party must keep confidential all information received from the other Party in connection with this Agreement and must not disclose or use such information except as permitted by this Agreement or with the other Party's prior written consent.

4.2 The obligations in clause 4.1 do not apply to information that is publicly available, independently developed, or required to be disclosed by law.

**5. Warranties**

5.1 The Provider warrants that:
(a) it has the right, power, and authority to enter into this Agreement and perform the Services;
(b) the Services will be performed with due care, skill, and diligence consistent with generally accepted industry standards;
(c) the Services and any deliverables will not infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party; and
(d) it holds all licenses, permits, and registrations required to perform the Services.

5.2 The Client warrants that it has the right, power, and authority to enter into this Agreement.

**6. Indemnity**

6.1 The Provider indemnifies the Client against all claims, losses, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable legal costs) arising from:
(a) a breach of this Agreement by the Provider;
(b) any negligent or wrongful act or omission of the Provider in performing the Services; or
(c) any infringement of a third party's intellectual property rights arising from the Services or any deliverables.

6.2 The Client indemnifies the Provider against all claims, losses, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable legal costs) arising from:
(a) a breach of this Agreement by the Client; or
(b) the Client's use of the Services or deliverables in a manner not contemplated by this Agreement.

**7. Limitation of Liability**

7.1 To the maximum extent permitted by law, neither Party's total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement (whether in contract, tort (including negligence), statute, or otherwise) exceeds [LIABILITY CAP, e.g., "the total Fees paid or payable under this Agreement in the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to the claim"].

7.2 Neither Party is liable for any indirect, consequential, special, or incidental loss or damage, including loss of profit, loss of revenue, loss of data, or loss of business opportunity.

7.3 Nothing in this Agreement limits liability for:
(a) death or personal injury caused by negligence;
(b) fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
(c) a breach of clause 4 (Confidentiality);
(d) the Provider's indemnity obligations under clause 6.1(c) (IP infringement); or
(e) any liability that cannot be limited or excluded by law.

**8. Term and Termination**

8.1 This Agreement commences on [COMMENCEMENT DATE] and continues for [INITIAL TERM] unless terminated earlier in accordance with this clause 8.

8.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement:
(a) for convenience by giving [NOTICE PERIOD, e.g., "thirty (30) days"] written notice to the other Party;
(b) immediately by written notice if the other Party commits a material breach that is not remedied within [CURE PERIOD, e.g., "fourteen (14) days"] of receiving notice of the breach; or
(c) immediately by written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, enters into administration, liquidation, or any arrangement with its creditors.

8.3 Upon termination, the Provider must deliver to the Client all completed and partially completed deliverables and all Client materials in the Provider's possession.

8.4 Termination does not affect accrued rights or obligations, and clauses 3, 4, 6, 7, and this clause 8.4 survive termination.

**9. General**

9.1 **Independent Contractor.** The Provider is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, or partner of the Client.

9.2 **Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of [GOVERNING JURISDICTION].

9.3 **Dispute Resolution.** Any dispute arising under this Agreement must first be referred to mediation administered by [MEDIATION BODY, e.g., "the Australian Disputes Centre"]. If mediation does not resolve the dispute within [MEDIATION PERIOD, e.g., "thirty (30) days"], either Party may commence court proceedings.

9.4 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement (including its Schedules) constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior agreements.

9.5 **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign its rights under this Agreement without the other Party's prior written consent, except that the Client may assign this Agreement to a related body corporate or in connection with a merger or acquisition.

9.6 **Notices.** All notices must be in writing and sent to the addresses specified in this Agreement or as updated by written notice.

**Schedule 1 — Services Description**

[DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF SERVICES, MILESTONES, DELIVERABLES, KEY PERSONNEL]

**Schedule 2 — Fees**

[FEE STRUCTURE: FIXED PRICE / TIME AND MATERIALS / RETAINER]
[PAYMENT SCHEDULE]
[EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT POLICY]

## Template 3: Employment Contract

### Purpose

An employment contract establishes the terms and conditions of employment between an employer and an employee. Unlike independent contractor agreements, employment contracts must comply with statutory minimum entitlements that cannot be contracted out of, including minimum wages, leave entitlements, unfair dismissal protections, and workplace health and safety obligations.

### Template Text

---

**EMPLOYMENT CONTRACT**

**Date:** [DATE]

**Parties:**

1. [EMPLOYER FULL LEGAL NAME] ([ACN/ABN/COMPANY NUMBER]) of [EMPLOYER ADDRESS] (**"Employer"**)
2. [EMPLOYEE FULL NAME] of [EMPLOYEE ADDRESS] (**"Employee"**)

**1. Position and Duties**

1.1 The Employer employs the Employee in the position of [POSITION TITLE], reporting to [REPORTING MANAGER TITLE].

1.2 The Employee's principal duties are set out in **Schedule 1**. The Employer may vary these duties from time to time, provided the varied duties are consistent with the Employee's skills, qualifications, and position.

1.3 The Employee must perform their duties faithfully, diligently, and to the best of their ability.

1.4 The Employee's principal place of work is [WORKPLACE ADDRESS]. [OPTIONAL: The Employer may require the Employee to work at other locations on reasonable notice.]

**2. Term and Probation**

2.1 Employment commences on [START DATE] and continues until terminated in accordance with this contract.

2.2 The first [PROBATION PERIOD, e.g., "six (6) months"] of employment is a probationary period. During the probationary period, either party may terminate employment by giving [PROBATION NOTICE, e.g., "one (1) week"] written notice.

**3. Remuneration**

3.1 The Employee's annual salary is [SALARY AMOUNT] (gross, before tax), payable [PAYMENT FREQUENCY, e.g., "fortnightly"] in arrears by electronic funds transfer.

3.2 [AU OPTION: The salary includes an amount equivalent to the minimum superannuation guarantee contribution required by the Superannuation Guarantee (Administration) Act 1992 (Cth). / The Employer will pay superannuation contributions in addition to the salary in accordance with applicable legislation.]

3.3 The Employer will review the Employee's salary annually but is not obliged to increase it.

**4. Hours of Work**

4.1 The Employee's ordinary hours of work are [HOURS, e.g., "38 hours per week, Monday to Friday"].

4.2 The Employee may be required to work reasonable additional hours to meet the requirements of the position. [AU: Refer to s 62 of the Fair Work Act 2009 regarding the right to refuse unreasonable additional hours.]

**5. Leave Entitlements**

5.1 The Employee is entitled to leave in accordance with applicable legislation, including:
(a) [AU: annual leave (4 weeks per year), personal/carer's leave (10 days per year), compassionate leave, parental leave, and long service leave as provided by the Fair Work Act 2009 and applicable state legislation]
[US: paid time off of [PTO DAYS] days per year, in accordance with the Employer's PTO policy]
[UK: annual leave (5.6 weeks per year, including bank holidays, per the Working Time Regulations 1998)]

**6. Confidentiality**

6.1 During and after employment, the Employee must not disclose or use the Employer's Confidential Information except as required in the proper performance of their duties.

6.2 **"Confidential Information"** means all information relating to the Employer's business, clients, finances, operations, technology, and strategies that is not publicly available.

**7. Intellectual Property**

7.1 All intellectual property created by the Employee in the course of employment belongs to the Employer. [AU: Copyright Act 1968 s 35(6); US: work-for-hire doctrine 17 USC s 101; UK: CDPA 1988 s 11(2).]

7.2 The Employee must promptly disclose all inventions, works, and designs created in connection with employment and must execute all documents necessary to vest title in the Employer.

**8. Post-Employment Restraints**

8.1 For a period of [RESTRAINT PERIOD, e.g., "six (6) months"] after the termination of employment, the Employee must not, within [GEOGRAPHIC AREA, e.g., "a 50 kilometer radius of the Employer's principal office"]:
(a) engage in or be concerned with any business that competes with the Employer's business;
(b) solicit or entice away any client or customer of the Employer; or
(c) solicit or recruit any employee of the Employer.

8.2 Each restraint in clause 8.1 is a separate and independent restraint. If any restraint is found to be unenforceable, it may be severed without affecting the validity of the remaining restraints.

8.3 [NOTE: Post-employment restraints are unenforceable in California per Bus & Prof Code s 16600. In other jurisdictions, enforceability depends on reasonableness of scope, duration, and geography.]

**9. Termination**

9.1 After the probationary period, either party may terminate employment by giving [NOTICE PERIOD, e.g., "four (4) weeks"] written notice, or, in the case of the Employer, payment in lieu of notice.

9.2 The Employer may terminate employment immediately without notice for serious misconduct, including fraud, dishonesty, serious breach of duty, or conduct that causes serious and imminent risk to health and safety.

9.3 Upon termination, the Employee must return all property of the Employer and delete all copies of Confidential Information.

**10. Governing Law**

10.1 This contract is governed by the laws of [GOVERNING JURISDICTION].

**Schedule 1 — Position Description**

[DETAILED DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES]

---

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Attempting to contract below statutory minimums.** Employment legislation sets floors that cannot be reduced by agreement. In Australia, the Fair Work Act 2009 and applicable Modern Awards set minimum wages, leave, and conditions. In the UK, the Employment Rights Act 1996 and Working Time Regulations 1998 set equivalent minimums.
2. **Misclassifying employees as contractors.** The legal consequences of misclassification include back-payment of entitlements, tax penalties, and potential personal liability for directors. See *Personnel Contracting* [2022] HCA 1 (AU); *Dynamex Operations West v Superior Court* 4 Cal.5th 903 (2018) (US).
3. **Unreasonable restraints of trade.** Restraints must be no broader than reasonably necessary to protect legitimate business interests. A restraint that prevents an employee from working in any capacity for any competitor anywhere for two years will almost certainly be struck down.

## Template 4: Residential Lease

### Purpose

A residential lease (tenancy agreement) grants a tenant the right to occupy residential premises for a defined period in exchange for rent. Residential tenancies are heavily regulated by statute in all covered jurisdictions.

### Template Text

---

**RESIDENTIAL TENANCY AGREEMENT**

**Date:** [DATE]

**Parties:**

1. [LANDLORD FULL LEGAL NAME] of [LANDLORD ADDRESS] (**"Landlord"**)
2. [TENANT FULL LEGAL NAME] of [TENANT CURRENT ADDRESS] (**"Tenant"**)

**1. Premises**

1.1 The Landlord grants the Tenant a tenancy of the premises at [PROPERTY ADDRESS] (the **"Premises"**), together with [INCLUDED ITEMS, e.g., "the fixtures, fittings, and chattels listed in the Condition Report"].

**2. Term**

2.1 The tenancy commences on [START DATE] and expires on [END DATE] (the **"Fixed Term"**), unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Agreement and applicable legislation.

2.2 At the expiry of the Fixed Term, the tenancy continues as a periodic tenancy [AU: on a month-to-month basis; UK: as a statutory periodic tenancy under s 5 Housing Act 1988] unless the parties enter into a new fixed-term agreement.

**3. Rent**

3.1 The Tenant must pay rent of [RENT AMOUNT] per [WEEK/FORTNIGHT/MONTH], payable in advance on [PAYMENT DAY] by [PAYMENT METHOD].

3.2 [AU: The Landlord must not increase rent during a fixed term of less than 2 years (Residential Tenancies Act 2010 (NSW) s 41). For periodic tenancies, at least 60 days' notice is required.]

3.3 [UK: The Landlord may increase rent with at least one month's notice using the section 13 Housing Act 1988 procedure, or as provided in any rent review clause.]

**4. Security Deposit (Bond)**

4.1 The Tenant must pay a security deposit of [BOND AMOUNT] before or on the commencement date.

4.2 [AU: The Landlord must lodge the bond with the relevant state bond authority (e.g., NSW Fair Trading, RTBA Victoria) within the time required by law.]

4.3 [UK: The Landlord must protect the deposit in a government-approved tenancy deposit scheme within 30 days of receipt (Housing Act 2004 ss 212-215).]

4.4 [US: The Landlord must hold the deposit in accordance with applicable state law, including any requirement for a separate trust account and interest payment.]

**5. Tenant's Obligations**

5.1 The Tenant must:
(a) pay rent on time;
(b) keep the Premises in a reasonable state of cleanliness and repair (fair wear and tear excepted);
(c) not make alterations or additions without the Landlord's prior written consent;
(d) not use the Premises for any illegal purpose;
(e) not cause or permit a nuisance;
(f) comply with all applicable strata or body corporate by-laws; and
(g) allow the Landlord access to the Premises for inspections and repairs with the notice required by applicable legislation.

**6. Landlord's Obligations**

6.1 The Landlord must:
(a) provide and maintain the Premises in a reasonable state of repair, having regard to the age and condition of the Premises at the commencement of the tenancy;
(b) comply with all applicable building, health, and safety laws;
(c) ensure the Premises are fit for habitation;
(d) not interfere with the Tenant's quiet enjoyment of the Premises; and
(e) pay all rates, taxes, and charges applicable to the Premises (except those the Tenant has agreed to pay).

**7. Termination**

7.1 Termination of this Agreement must comply with applicable residential tenancy legislation, including the minimum notice periods prescribed by law.

7.2 [AU: Refer to the applicable state Residential Tenancies Act for notice periods. NSW: 30 days for end of fixed term (s 84); 90 days no-grounds for periodic (to be amended under reforms).]

7.3 [UK: The Landlord must comply with the section 21 (no-fault) or section 8 (fault-based) procedures under the Housing Act 1988, noting pending Renters' Reform Bill changes.]

7.4 [US: Notice periods and permissible grounds for termination vary by state. Consult applicable landlord-tenant statute.]

**8. Condition Report**

8.1 Before or at the commencement of the tenancy, the parties must complete a condition report recording the condition of the Premises and all inclusions. [AU: This is mandatory under residential tenancy legislation in all states.]

**9. Governing Law**

9.1 This Agreement is governed by [APPLICABLE RESIDENTIAL TENANCY LEGISLATION AND GOVERNING JURISDICTION].

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Attempting to contract out of residential tenancy legislation.** In most jurisdictions, the statutory protections for tenants cannot be excluded or modified by agreement. Clauses that purport to do so are void.
2. **Failing to lodge the bond/deposit.** In Australia, failing to lodge the bond is an offense. In the UK, failing to protect the deposit prevents the landlord from serving a valid section 21 notice and exposes the landlord to a penalty of one to three times the deposit amount.
3. **No condition report.** Without a condition report, the landlord may be unable to claim against the bond for damage at the end of the tenancy.
4. **Prohibited fees.** The Tenant Fees Act 2019 (UK) prohibits most fees charged to tenants. Similar restrictions exist in other jurisdictions. Charging prohibited fees may void the clause or create liability.
