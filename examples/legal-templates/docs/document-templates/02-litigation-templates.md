# Litigation Templates

**The Codex** — Business, Finance & Law Division
**Section:** AF-PRAC-03-02
**Document Type:** Template Collection
**Jurisdiction Coverage:** AU, US, UK, CA, NZ

**Paper section references:** [`uk-moj-n1-claim-form.pdf`](uk-moj-n1-claim-form.pdf), [`uk-n1a-notes-for-claimant.pdf`](uk-n1a-notes-for-claimant.pdf)

## Overview

This document provides templates for the core documents used in litigation: statement of claim, defense, subpoena, notice of motion, and affidavit of service. Each template includes court-specific formatting requirements, jurisdiction-specific form numbers, filing deadlines, and service requirements.

Litigation documents must comply with the applicable rules of court. Unlike transactional documents where the parties have substantial freedom in format and content, court documents must follow prescribed forms and comply with detailed procedural rules. Non-compliance can result in the document being struck out, costs orders, or dismissal of the proceedings.

**Critical Note:** Court forms and rules change frequently. Always verify current form numbers, filing fees, and procedural requirements against the relevant court's website before filing.

## Template 1: Statement of Claim

### Purpose

A statement of claim (called a complaint in the US, a claim form and particulars of claim in the UK) is the originating process by which a plaintiff commences civil proceedings. It sets out the material facts relied upon, the legal basis for the claim, and the relief sought. It must be filed with the court and served on the defendant within the time prescribed by the rules.

### Court-Specific Form Numbers

| Jurisdiction | Form | Rules Reference |
|-------------|------|-----------------|
| NSW (Supreme/District) | Form 6 UCPR | Uniform Civil Procedure Rules 2005 (NSW) r 6.2 |
| NSW (Local Court) | Form 2 LCR | Local Court Rules 2009 |
| Federal Court (AU) | Form 17 | Federal Court Rules 2011 r 8.21 |
| Victoria (Supreme) | Form 5A | Supreme Court (General Civil Procedure) Rules 2015 r 5.02 |
| US (Federal) | No prescribed form; must comply with FRCP | Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, Rule 8 |
| England and Wales | Form N1 (claim form) + Particulars of Claim | Civil Procedure Rules 1998, Part 7 |
| Canada (Ontario) | Form 14A | Rules of Civil Procedure, Rule 14.03 |
| New Zealand | Form G9 (High Court) | High Court Rules 2016, r 5.26 |

### Filing Time Limits (Limitation Periods)

| Cause of Action | AU (NSW) | US (Federal/General) | UK | CA (ON) | NZ |
|----------------|----------|---------------------|----|---------|----|
| Contract | 6 years (Limitation Act 1969 s 14) | Varies by state (typically 4-6 years) | 6 years (Limitation Act 1980 s 5) | 2 years (Limitations Act 2002 s 4) | 6 years (Limitation Act 2010 s 11) |
| Tort (general) | 6 years (s 14) | Varies (typically 2-3 years) | 6 years (s 2) | 2 years (s 4) | 6 years (s 11) |
| Personal injury | 3 years from discoverability (s 50C) | Varies (typically 2-3 years) | 3 years (s 11) | 2 years (s 4) | 3 years from discoverability (ACC regime applies) |
| Defamation | 1 year, extendable to 3 (s 14B) | Varies (typically 1-2 years) | 1 year (s 4A) | 2 years (s 4) | 2 years (Defamation Act 1992 s 55) |

### Template Text

---

**IN THE [COURT NAME]**
**OF [STATE/JURISDICTION]**

**[REGISTRY/DIVISION]**

**Case No.:** [TO BE ASSIGNED ON FILING]

**BETWEEN:**

[PLAINTIFF FULL LEGAL NAME]
[ACN/ABN IF APPLICABLE]
Plaintiff

**AND**

[DEFENDANT FULL LEGAL NAME]
[ACN/ABN IF APPLICABLE]
[FIRST/SECOND] Defendant

---

**STATEMENT OF CLAIM**

Filed: [DATE]

**The Plaintiff claims:**

1. Damages in the amount of [AMOUNT OR "to be assessed"].
2. Interest pursuant to [APPLICABLE INTEREST STATUTE, e.g., "section 100 of the Civil Procedure Act 2005 (NSW)"].
3. Costs.
4. [ANY FURTHER OR OTHER RELIEF, e.g., "An injunction restraining the Defendant from..."].

---

**PLEADINGS**

**A. The Parties**

1. The Plaintiff is [DESCRIPTION, e.g., "a company incorporated under the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) and carrying on business as a software developer"].

2. The [First] Defendant is [DESCRIPTION, e.g., "a company incorporated under the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) and carrying on business as a technology consultant"].

3. [IF APPLICABLE: The Second Defendant is...]

**B. Background Facts**

4. On or about [DATE], the Plaintiff and the Defendant entered into a [TYPE OF AGREEMENT] (the **"Agreement"**), a copy of which is annexed hereto and marked **"A"**.

5. [SET OUT MATERIAL FACTS IN NUMBERED PARAGRAPHS. EACH PARAGRAPH SHOULD CONTAIN ONE MATERIAL FACT.]

6. Pursuant to the Agreement, the Defendant was obliged to [SET OUT CONTRACTUAL OBLIGATION].

7. [CONTINUE WITH CHRONOLOGICAL NARRATIVE OF MATERIAL FACTS.]

**C. Breach / Cause of Action**

8. The Defendant breached the Agreement in that [PARTICULARS OF BREACH]:
   (a) [FIRST PARTICULAR OF BREACH];
   (b) [SECOND PARTICULAR OF BREACH]; and
   (c) [FURTHER PARTICULARS].

*Particulars*
[PROVIDE SPECIFIC DETAILS OF WHEN, HOW, AND IN WHAT RESPECT THE BREACH OCCURRED.]

9. [ALTERNATIVE OR ADDITIONAL CAUSE OF ACTION, e.g.:]
   Further or in the alternative, the Defendant engaged in misleading or deceptive conduct in contravention of [section 18 of the Australian Consumer Law / section 5 of the Federal Trade Commission Act / section 2(1) of the Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008 (UK)] in that [PARTICULARS].

**D. Loss and Damage**

10. By reason of the matters set out above, the Plaintiff has suffered loss and damage.

*Particulars of Loss and Damage*
(a) [CATEGORY 1, e.g., "Cost of rectification works: $[AMOUNT]"]
(b) [CATEGORY 2, e.g., "Loss of revenue for the period [DATE] to [DATE]: $[AMOUNT]"]
(c) [CATEGORY 3, e.g., "Wasted expenditure: $[AMOUNT]"]
(d) Further particulars will be provided prior to trial.

---

**Filed for the Plaintiff by:**

[SOLICITOR NAME]
[FIRM NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[TELEPHONE]
[EMAIL]
[REFERENCE]

### Key Content Requirements

| Element | Requirement | Consequence of Omission |
|---------|-------------|------------------------|
| Material facts | Must plead all facts essential to the cause of action | Claim may be struck out as disclosing no reasonable cause of action |
| Particulars | Must provide sufficient detail for the defendant to understand the case | Court may order further and better particulars; may strike out |
| Relief sought | Must specify the relief claimed | Court cannot grant relief not claimed |
| Verification | Some jurisdictions require verification by the plaintiff | Document may be rejected for filing |
| Annexures | Must identify and annex key documents relied upon | May need to amend to include later |

### Service Requirements

| Jurisdiction | Method | Time Limit After Filing |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| NSW | Personal or substituted service (UCPR r 10.20) | Valid for 6 months (UCPR r 6.2(4)) |
| Federal Court (AU) | Personal, substituted, or agreed method (FCR r 10.01) | Valid for 12 months |
| US (Federal) | Per FRCP Rule 4 (personal, agent, mail with waiver) | 90 days (FRCP r 4(m)) |
| England and Wales | Per CPR r 6.3 (personal, first class post, DX, fax, email) | 4 months (domestic); 6 months (overseas) |
| Ontario | Personal or alternative (Rule 16) | 6 months |
| New Zealand | Personal or substituted (HCR r 6.1) | 12 months |

## Template 2: Defense (and Counterclaim)

### Purpose

A defense is the defendant's response to the statement of claim. It must address each allegation in the claim, either admitting it, denying it, or stating that the defendant does not admit the allegation (putting the plaintiff to proof). A counterclaim may be filed with the defense if the defendant has claims against the plaintiff.

### Filing Time Limits

| Jurisdiction | Time to File Defense |
|-------------|---------------------|
| NSW | 28 days after service (UCPR r 14.3) |
| Federal Court (AU) | 28 days after service (FCR r 16.32) |
| US (Federal) | 21 days after service; 60 days if service waived (FRCP r 12(a)) |
| England and Wales | 14 days after service of particulars; 28 days with acknowledgment of service (CPR r 15.4) |
| Ontario | 20 days (local); 40 days (outside province); 60 days (outside country) (Rule 18.01) |
| New Zealand | 25 working days (HCR r 5.53) |

### Template Text

---

**IN THE [COURT NAME]**
**OF [STATE/JURISDICTION]**

**Case No.:** [CASE NUMBER]

**BETWEEN:**

[PLAINTIFF NAME]
Plaintiff

**AND**

[DEFENDANT NAME]
Defendant

---

**DEFENSE [AND COUNTERCLAIM]**

Filed: [DATE]

**A. Response to Statement of Claim**

1. As to paragraph 1 of the Statement of Claim, the Defendant admits [that portion admitted] and [denies / does not admit] [that portion denied].

2. As to paragraph 2 of the Statement of Claim, the Defendant [admits / denies / does not admit] [the allegation].

3. [CONTINUE RESPONDING TO EACH PARAGRAPH OF THE STATEMENT OF CLAIM.]

4. As to paragraph [X] of the Statement of Claim, the Defendant denies that [specific allegation]. The Defendant says that [positive case in response, with particulars].

*Particulars*
[PROVIDE SPECIFIC FACTS RELIED UPON IN SUPPORT OF THE DENIAL.]

**B. Affirmative Defenses**

5. Further and in the alternative, the Defendant says the Plaintiff's claim is barred by [AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE, e.g.]:
   (a) the Limitation Act [YEAR] in that the cause of action accrued on [DATE], more than [X] years before the commencement of proceedings;
   (b) contributory negligence on the part of the Plaintiff, in that [PARTICULARS];
   (c) the Plaintiff's failure to mitigate its loss, in that [PARTICULARS];
   (d) [FURTHER AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES].

**C. Counterclaim** [IF APPLICABLE]

6. The Defendant counterclaims against the Plaintiff as follows.

7. [SET OUT COUNTERCLAIM FOLLOWING THE SAME FORMAT AS A STATEMENT OF CLAIM, WITH MATERIAL FACTS, CAUSE OF ACTION, AND RELIEF SOUGHT.]

8. The Defendant claims on the Counterclaim:
   (a) Damages in the amount of [AMOUNT OR "to be assessed"].
   (b) Interest.
   (c) Costs.

---

**Filed for the Defendant by:**

[SOLICITOR NAME]
[FIRM NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[TELEPHONE]
[EMAIL]
[REFERENCE]

## Template 3: Subpoena

### Purpose

A subpoena is a court order requiring a person to produce documents (subpoena to produce / *subpoena duces tecum*), attend to give evidence (subpoena to attend / *subpoena ad testificandum*), or both. Subpoenas compel third parties who are not parties to the proceedings to provide documents or evidence relevant to the dispute.

### Template Text — Subpoena to Produce

---

**IN THE [COURT NAME]**
**OF [STATE/JURISDICTION]**

**Case No.:** [CASE NUMBER]

**BETWEEN:**

[PLAINTIFF NAME]
Plaintiff

**AND**

[DEFENDANT NAME]
Defendant

---

**SUBPOENA TO PRODUCE**

**To:** [ADDRESSEE FULL NAME AND ADDRESS]

**You are ordered** to produce to the Court at [COURT ADDRESS] on [RETURN DATE] at [TIME] the documents and things described in the Schedule below.

**You must produce these documents** by the date shown above. You need not attend personally if you produce the documents to the Court registry by that date.

**Failure to comply** with this subpoena without lawful excuse is a contempt of court and may result in a fine or imprisonment.

**Schedule — Documents to be Produced**

1. All [DESCRIPTION OF DOCUMENTS, e.g., "correspondence, emails, and file notes"] between [PARTIES TO COMMUNICATIONS] relating to [SUBJECT MATTER] for the period [START DATE] to [END DATE].

2. All [DESCRIPTION, e.g., "financial records including invoices, receipts, and bank statements"] relating to [SUBJECT MATTER] for the period [START DATE] to [END DATE].

3. [CONTINUE WITH SPECIFIC, IDENTIFIED CATEGORIES OF DOCUMENTS.]

---

**Issued at the request of:**

[SOLICITOR NAME]
[FIRM NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[TELEPHONE]
[EMAIL]
[REFERENCE]

---

### Key Requirements for Subpoenas

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Specificity | Must describe documents with reasonable specificity; cannot be a "fishing expedition" |
| Relevance | Documents sought must be relevant to an issue in the proceedings |
| Legitimate forensic purpose | Must serve a legitimate forensic purpose, not be oppressive or used for discovery against a non-party |
| Conduct money | AU: Must be served with conduct money (reasonable cost of compliance). US: FRCP r 45(d)(2) — reasonable costs. UK: Reasonable costs must be tendered. |
| Leave requirement | Some courts require leave to issue a subpoena to a non-party |
| Objection | The addressee may object on grounds of privilege, oppression, or lack of legitimate forensic purpose |

## Template 4: Notice of Motion

### Purpose

A notice of motion is the document used to bring interlocutory (interim) applications before the court during the course of proceedings. Common interlocutory applications include applications for injunctions, security for costs, summary judgment, strike-out of pleadings, discovery orders, and adjournments.

### Template Text

---

**IN THE [COURT NAME]**
**OF [STATE/JURISDICTION]**

**Case No.:** [CASE NUMBER]

**BETWEEN:**

[PLAINTIFF NAME]
Plaintiff

**AND**

[DEFENDANT NAME]
Defendant

---

**NOTICE OF MOTION**

Filed: [DATE]

**To the [Plaintiff/Defendant] and the Court:**

The [Plaintiff/Defendant] will apply to the Court at [COURT ADDRESS] on [DATE] at [TIME] or as soon thereafter as counsel can be heard for the following orders:

**Orders Sought:**

1. [FIRST ORDER SOUGHT, e.g., "That the Defendant's defense filed on [DATE] be struck out pursuant to [UCPR r 14.28 / FRCP r 12(f) / CPR r 3.4]."]

2. [SECOND ORDER SOUGHT, e.g., "That the Defendant provide security for the Plaintiff's costs in the amount of $[AMOUNT] pursuant to [Corporations Act 2001 s 1335 / FRCP r 65 / CPR r 25.12]."]

3. [FURTHER ORDERS SOUGHT.]

4. Costs of and incidental to this motion.

5. Such further or other orders as the Court sees fit.

**Grounds:**

This application is made on the grounds that:

1. [FIRST GROUND, e.g., "The defense discloses no reasonable defense to the claim."]

2. [SECOND GROUND, e.g., "The Defendant is a corporation and there is reason to believe that it will be unable to pay the Plaintiff's costs if ordered to do so."]

3. [FURTHER GROUNDS.]

**Evidence:**

This application is supported by:

1. The affidavit of [DEPONENT NAME] sworn/affirmed [DATE].
2. [FURTHER AFFIDAVITS OR EVIDENCE.]

**Time Estimate:** [ESTIMATED HEARING TIME, e.g., "Half day"]

---

**Filed for the [Plaintiff/Defendant] by:**

[SOLICITOR NAME]
[FIRM NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[TELEPHONE]
[EMAIL]
[REFERENCE]

## Template 5: Affidavit of Service

### Purpose

An affidavit of service is a sworn statement (or affirmed statement) by the person who served a document, confirming when, where, how, and on whom the document was served. It provides evidence to the court that procedural service requirements have been satisfied. This is critical because a court generally cannot make orders against a party who has not been served.

### Template Text

---

**IN THE [COURT NAME]**
**OF [STATE/JURISDICTION]**

**Case No.:** [CASE NUMBER]

**BETWEEN:**

[PLAINTIFF NAME]
Plaintiff

**AND**

[DEFENDANT NAME]
Defendant

---

**AFFIDAVIT OF SERVICE**

I, [SERVER'S FULL NAME], of [SERVER'S ADDRESS], [OCCUPATION], [swear/affirm]:

1. On [DATE OF SERVICE] at approximately [TIME OF SERVICE], I served [DESCRIPTION OF DOCUMENT(S) SERVED, e.g., "the Statement of Claim and this Affidavit of Service"] on [PERSON SERVED'S FULL NAME] by [METHOD OF SERVICE]:

   [SELECT APPLICABLE METHOD:]

   **(a) Personal Service:** I personally delivered a sealed copy of the document(s) to [PERSON SERVED] at [ADDRESS WHERE SERVICE EFFECTED]. I identified [PERSON SERVED] by [METHOD OF IDENTIFICATION, e.g., "asking them to confirm their name," "recognizing them from a photograph provided to me by the solicitor for the Plaintiff"].

   **(b) Substituted Service:** Pursuant to the order of [JUDGE/REGISTRAR NAME] made on [DATE OF ORDER], I served the document(s) by [METHOD AUTHORIZED BY ORDER, e.g., "sending a copy by registered post to [ADDRESS]" / "sending a copy by email to [EMAIL ADDRESS]" / "leaving a copy affixed to the front door of [ADDRESS]"].

   **(c) Service on Corporation:** I served the document(s) on [CORPORATION NAME] by [METHOD, e.g., "leaving a copy at the registered office at [ADDRESS]" / "posting a copy by prepaid post to the registered office at [ADDRESS]"] in accordance with [section 109X of the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) / applicable corporate service provision].

2. At the time of service, the person I served appeared to be over the age of 18 years.

3. Annexed hereto and marked **"A"** is a true copy of the document(s) served.

[IF APPLICABLE:]
4. I was unable to effect personal service because [REASONS]. I attempted personal service on the following occasions:
   (a) [DATE, TIME, LOCATION, RESULT];
   (b) [DATE, TIME, LOCATION, RESULT]; and
   (c) [DATE, TIME, LOCATION, RESULT].

**[Sworn/Affirmed]** at [PLACE] on [DATE]

Before me:

________________________
[JUSTICE OF THE PEACE / SOLICITOR / NOTARY PUBLIC / COMMISSIONER FOR OATHS]
[NAME AND QUALIFICATION]

________________________
[DEPONENT'S SIGNATURE]
[DEPONENT'S NAME]

### Formatting Rules by Jurisdiction

| Requirement | AU | US | UK |
|-------------|----|----|-----|
| Oath/Affirmation | Either (UCPR r 35.2) | Either (varies by state) | Statement of truth (CPR PD 22); or affidavit in limited cases |
| Witness qualification | JP, solicitor, or authorized person | Notary public, court clerk | Solicitor, commissioner for oaths |
| Exhibits/Annexures | Numbered and identified by exhibit certificate | Numbered and attached | Exhibit certificate required |
| Jurat form | "Sworn/Affirmed at [place] on [date]" | Varies by state | "Sworn/Affirmed at [place] on [date]" |
| Filing | Filed with court registry | Filed with clerk of court | Filed at court office |

## Common Pitfalls Across All Litigation Templates

1. **Incorrect form number or format.** Each court has specific form requirements. Using the wrong form or non-compliant formatting can result in rejection at the registry.

2. **Missing verification or certificate of truth.** Some jurisdictions require a verification statement or certificate confirming the truth of the contents. The UK requires a statement of truth on most documents; failure to include it renders the document a nullity.

3. **Defective service.** Service that does not comply with the rules is not valid service, and any judgment obtained may be set aside. Always confirm current service rules and document compliance meticulously.

4. **Time limit miscalculation.** Limitation periods and filing deadlines are strict. In many jurisdictions, the day of the event is excluded and the last day of the period must be a business day. Always calculate deadlines conservatively and calendar them with sufficient lead time.

5. **Insufficient particulars.** A statement of claim that pleads conclusions rather than material facts will be struck out. State the facts; let the court draw the legal conclusions.

6. **Subpoena as discovery substitute.** A subpoena to a non-party cannot be used as a substitute for discovery between the parties. Courts will set aside subpoenas that amount to an impermissible fishing expedition.
