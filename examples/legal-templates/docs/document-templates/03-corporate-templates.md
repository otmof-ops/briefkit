## Overview

This document provides templates for core corporate governance documents: company constitution, shareholder agreement, directors' circular resolution, and meeting minutes. Each template includes statutory requirements, key clauses, ASIC and equivalent filing requirements, and jurisdiction-specific variations.

Corporate documents operate within a statutory framework that varies significantly across jurisdictions. In Australia, the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) provides a comprehensive code supplemented by replaceable rules that apply unless displaced by a constitution. In the United States, state corporation laws (principally the Delaware General Corporation Law) provide the framework, supplemented by the certificate of incorporation and bylaws. In the United Kingdom, the Companies Act 2006 provides the governing framework, with model articles of association applying by default.

## Template 1: Company Constitution

### Purpose

A company constitution (articles of association in the UK, bylaws in the US) is the internal governing document of a corporation. It sets out the rules for the company's internal management, including share rights, director powers, meeting procedures, and dividend policy. In Australia, proprietary companies may either adopt the replaceable rules in the Corporations Act 2001 or adopt a custom constitution that displaces some or all of those rules.

### Replaceable Rules vs Custom Constitution (AU)

| Factor | Replaceable Rules | Custom Constitution |
|--------|------------------|---------------------|
| Source | Corporations Act 2001 ss 135-140; specific sections marked as replaceable rules | Drafted and adopted by the company |
| Default | Apply automatically unless displaced | Must be adopted by special resolution (s 136) |
| Single-member companies | Do not apply (s 135(1)(a)) | Required for single-member proprietary companies |
| Amendment | Cannot be amended (they are statutory provisions) | Amended by special resolution (75% of votes cast: s 136(2)) |
| ASIC filing | No filing required | Must be lodged with ASIC (s 136(5)) |
| Customization | No | Yes — can tailor share classes, pre-emptive rights, dispute mechanisms |

### Template Text

---

**CONSTITUTION**
**of**
**[COMPANY NAME] [PTY LTD / LIMITED]**

**ACN [COMPANY ACN]**

Adopted by special resolution on [DATE].

---

**1. Definitions and Interpretation**

1.1 In this Constitution:

**"Act"** means the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) as amended from time to time.

**"Board"** means the board of directors of the Company.

**"Company"** means [COMPANY NAME] [PTY LTD / LIMITED] ACN [ACN].

**"Constitution"** means this constitution as amended from time to time.

**"Director"** means a person appointed as a director of the Company.

**"Member"** means a person registered as a holder of Shares.

**"Ordinary Resolution"** means a resolution passed by a simple majority of votes cast.

**"Share"** means a share in the capital of the Company.

**"Special Resolution"** has the meaning given in section 9 of the Act.

1.2 Unless the context requires otherwise, words defined in the Act have the same meaning in this Constitution.

1.3 If there is any inconsistency between this Constitution and the Act, the Act prevails to the extent of the inconsistency.

1.4 The replaceable rules in the Act do not apply to the Company.

**2. Share Capital**

2.1 The Company may issue Shares in one or more classes, with such rights, privileges, and restrictions as the Board determines, subject to the Act and this Constitution.

2.2 [OPTIONAL — PRE-EMPTIVE RIGHTS:] Before issuing any new Shares, the Board must first offer those Shares to existing Members in proportion to their existing shareholdings, at the same price and on the same terms as the proposed issue. Each Member has [OFFER PERIOD, e.g., "twenty-one (21) days"] to accept or decline the offer. Only Shares not taken up by existing Members may be issued to other persons.

2.3 The Company must not issue shares to a person unless the Board has first obtained the approval of existing Members by Ordinary Resolution, except where the issue is:
(a) an issue to existing Members in proportion to their existing holdings; or
(b) permitted by the Act without member approval.

**3. Transfer of Shares**

3.1 Subject to this Constitution, Shares are transferable.

3.2 [PTY LTD RESTRICTION:] The Board may refuse to register any transfer of Shares and is not required to provide reasons for the refusal. [NOTE: For proprietary companies (AU), the Corporations Act 2001 s 113(1) requires restrictions on share transfer.]

3.3 [OPTIONAL — RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL:] A Member who wishes to transfer Shares (the **"Selling Member"**) must first offer those Shares to the other Members by written notice specifying the number of Shares, the proposed price, and the terms of sale. The other Members have [OFFER PERIOD, e.g., "twenty-eight (28) days"] to accept the offer in proportion to their existing holdings. If the other Members do not acquire all the offered Shares, the Selling Member may transfer the remaining Shares to a third party at a price no less than the offered price, within [TRANSFER PERIOD, e.g., "sixty (60) days"].

**4. Directors**

4.1 The Company must have at least [MINIMUM DIRECTORS, e.g., "one (1) director" for a proprietary company; "three (3) directors" for a public company] who is an individual and at least [NUMBER] of whom must ordinarily reside in Australia. [NOTE: s 201A Corporations Act 2001.]

4.2 Directors are appointed and removed by Ordinary Resolution of the Members, or as otherwise provided by this Constitution and the Act.

4.3 The Board may appoint a person as an additional Director to hold office until the next general meeting.

4.4 A Director may resign by written notice to the Company.

**5. Powers and Duties of Directors**

5.1 The business of the Company is managed by or under the direction of the Board.

5.2 The Board may exercise all powers of the Company that are not required by the Act or this Constitution to be exercised by the Members.

5.3 Directors must comply with their duties under the Act, including the duties in sections 180-184 (duties of care, good faith, proper purpose, no improper use of position or information).

**6. Directors' Meetings**

6.1 The Board may meet, adjourn, and otherwise regulate its meetings as it sees fit.

6.2 A Director may call a meeting of the Board by giving reasonable notice to all other Directors.

6.3 The quorum for a meeting of the Board is [QUORUM, e.g., "a majority of Directors entitled to vote"].

6.4 Questions arising at a meeting are decided by a majority of votes. The chairperson has a casting vote in the event of an equality of votes. [OPTIONAL: Omit casting vote for closely held companies.]

6.5 A meeting of the Board may be held using any technology that allows each Director to communicate with every other Director simultaneously. A Director who participates in a meeting by such technology is taken to be present at the meeting.

**7. Circular Resolutions of Directors**

7.1 The Board may pass a resolution without a meeting if all Directors entitled to vote sign a document containing a statement that they are in favor of the resolution set out in the document. [NOTE: s 248A Corporations Act 2001.]

7.2 Separate copies of the document may be signed by different Directors. The resolution is passed when the last Director signs.

**8. Members' Meetings**

8.1 The Company must hold an annual general meeting within [TIMEFRAME PER ACT, e.g., "five (5) months after the end of its financial year"], unless exempted under the Act. [NOTE: Proprietary companies are generally exempted from holding AGMs: s 250N.]

8.2 The Board may call a general meeting at any time. Members with at least 5% of the votes may requisition a meeting under section 249D of the Act.

8.3 At least [NOTICE PERIOD, e.g., "twenty-one (21) days for a special resolution; twenty-eight (28) days for an AGM of a listed company"] written notice must be given.

8.4 The quorum for a general meeting is [QUORUM, e.g., "two (2) Members present in person or by proxy holding at least [PERCENTAGE]% of the issued Shares"].

**9. Dividends**

9.1 The Board may determine that a dividend is payable and fix the amount, the time for payment, and the method of payment.

9.2 No dividend may be paid except out of profits of the Company. [NOTE: s 254T Corporations Act 2001 — dividends may only be paid if the company's assets exceed its liabilities, payment is fair and reasonable, and payment does not materially prejudice the company's ability to pay creditors.]

**10. Winding Up**

10.1 If the Company is wound up, the liquidator may, with the sanction of a Special Resolution, distribute among the Members in kind any part of the Company's property.

10.2 Subject to the rights of any holders of Shares issued on special terms, on a winding up the assets of the Company remaining after payment of debts are distributed among the Members in proportion to their shareholdings.

**11. Indemnity and Insurance**

11.1 To the maximum extent permitted by the Act, the Company indemnifies each officer (as defined in the Act) against all liabilities incurred in their capacity as an officer, except liability to the Company or a related body corporate, or liability arising from conduct involving a lack of good faith.

11.2 The Company may pay premiums for insurance against liability for officers to the extent permitted by the Act. [NOTE: ss 199A-199C Corporations Act 2001.]

**12. Amendment**

12.1 This Constitution may be amended only by Special Resolution passed at a general meeting of Members in accordance with section 136(2) of the Act.

## Template 2: Shareholder Agreement

### Purpose

A shareholder agreement is a contract between the shareholders of a company that supplements the constitution. It governs matters such as decision-making, share transfer restrictions, dividend policy, exit mechanisms, and dispute resolution. Unlike a constitution, a shareholder agreement is a private contract that is not filed with any regulatory authority and is not publicly available.

### Template Text

---

**SHAREHOLDER AGREEMENT**

**Date:** [DATE]

**Parties:**

1. [COMPANY FULL LEGAL NAME] ([ACN/COMPANY NUMBER]) of [REGISTERED ADDRESS] (**"Company"**)
2. [SHAREHOLDER 1 NAME] of [ADDRESS] (**"Shareholder 1"**)
3. [SHAREHOLDER 2 NAME] of [ADDRESS] (**"Shareholder 2"**)
4. [ADDITIONAL SHAREHOLDERS AS REQUIRED]

(each a **"Shareholder"** and together the **"Shareholders"**)

**Background**

A. The Company is a [TYPE] company with issued share capital as set out in Schedule 1.

B. The Shareholders wish to regulate their rights and obligations in relation to the Company on the terms of this Agreement.

**1. Conduct of Business**

1.1 The Company's business is [DESCRIPTION OF BUSINESS] (the **"Business"**).

1.2 The Shareholders must exercise their powers as shareholders and procure that Directors appointed by them exercise their powers to ensure the Company carries on the Business in accordance with this Agreement.

**2. Board Composition**

2.1 The Board will consist of [NUMBER] Directors.

2.2 [SHAREHOLDER 1] is entitled to appoint [NUMBER] Directors.

2.3 [SHAREHOLDER 2] is entitled to appoint [NUMBER] Directors.

2.4 [OPTIONAL: The Shareholders must jointly appoint [NUMBER] independent Director(s).]

2.5 A Shareholder may remove and replace a Director appointed by that Shareholder at any time by written notice to the Company.

**3. Reserved Matters**

3.1 The Company must not take any of the following actions without the prior written consent of Shareholders holding at least [THRESHOLD, e.g., "75%"] of the issued Shares (or the unanimous consent of all Shareholders, where indicated):

(a) amend the Constitution;
(b) issue new Shares or grant options over Shares;
(c) declare or pay a dividend;
(d) incur borrowings exceeding [THRESHOLD AMOUNT];
(e) enter into any contract with a value exceeding [THRESHOLD AMOUNT];
(f) appoint or remove the CEO or other key management personnel;
(g) change the nature of the Business;
(h) commence or settle litigation with a value exceeding [THRESHOLD AMOUNT];
(i) wind up or deregister the Company (unanimous consent); or
(j) admit a new Shareholder (unanimous consent).

**4. Pre-Emptive Rights on Share Issues**

4.1 Before issuing any new Shares, the Company must first offer them to existing Shareholders in proportion to their existing shareholdings, at the same price and on the same terms, with an acceptance period of [PERIOD, e.g., "twenty-one (21) days"].

**5. Transfer Restrictions**

5.1 A Shareholder must not transfer any Shares except in accordance with this clause 5.

5.2 **Right of First Refusal.** Before transferring Shares, the Selling Shareholder must offer them to the remaining Shareholders in proportion to their holdings, at the proposed transfer price, with an acceptance period of [PERIOD].

5.3 **Tag-Along Rights.** If a Shareholder receives an offer to acquire [THRESHOLD, e.g., "50% or more"] of the issued Shares, each other Shareholder has the right to sell their Shares to the offeror on the same terms and at the same price.

5.4 **Drag-Along Rights.** If Shareholders holding [THRESHOLD, e.g., "75% or more"] of the issued Shares accept an offer for all Shares, the remaining Shareholders must sell their Shares to the offeror on the same terms.

**6. Deadlock**

6.1 If the Board or Shareholders are unable to reach agreement on a matter requiring their approval within [PERIOD, e.g., "thirty (30) days"] of the matter first being raised (a **"Deadlock"**), the following procedure applies:

(a) The Shareholders must first attempt to resolve the Deadlock through good faith negotiation between their respective [CEO/Managing Director/Senior Representative].

(b) If not resolved within [PERIOD], the matter must be referred to mediation.

(c) If mediation does not resolve the Deadlock within [PERIOD], either Shareholder may invoke the buy-sell mechanism in clause 7.

**7. Buy-Sell (Shotgun) Mechanism**

7.1 Following a Deadlock that is not resolved under clause 6, any Shareholder (the **"Offeror"**) may serve a notice on the other Shareholders (each an **"Offeree"**) specifying a price per Share (the **"Offer Price"**).

7.2 Within [PERIOD, e.g., "thirty (30) days"], each Offeree must elect to either:
(a) sell all of its Shares to the Offeror at the Offer Price; or
(b) purchase all of the Offeror's Shares at the Offer Price.

7.3 If an Offeree fails to elect within the period, the Offeree is deemed to have elected to sell.

**8. Dividend Policy**

8.1 Subject to the Act and the Company's financial position, the Company will distribute [PERCENTAGE, e.g., "not less than 50%"] of its annual after-tax profits as dividends, unless the Shareholders unanimously agree otherwise.

**9. Restraint of Trade**

9.1 During the term of this Agreement and for [PERIOD, e.g., "twelve (12) months"] after ceasing to be a Shareholder, each Shareholder must not directly or indirectly engage in any business that competes with the Business within [GEOGRAPHIC AREA].

**10. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution**

10.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of [GOVERNING JURISDICTION].

10.2 Disputes must be referred to mediation. If unresolved, disputes must be determined by arbitration under the [ARBITRATION RULES, e.g., "ACICA Arbitration Rules"].

**Schedule 1 — Shareholdings**

| Shareholder | Shares | Class | Percentage |
|-------------|--------|-------|------------|
| [SHAREHOLDER 1] | [NUMBER] | Ordinary | [%] |
| [SHAREHOLDER 2] | [NUMBER] | Ordinary | [%] |

## Template 3: Directors' Resolution (Circular)

### Purpose

A circular resolution allows directors to pass a resolution without meeting, provided all directors entitled to vote sign the resolution. This is the most common method of passing routine board decisions in small to medium companies.

### Template Text

---

**[COMPANY NAME] [PTY LTD / LIMITED]**
**ACN [COMPANY ACN]**

**CIRCULAR RESOLUTION OF DIRECTORS**

Pursuant to [section 248A of the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) / clause [X] of the Company's Constitution / section 141 of the Companies Act 2006 (UK) / section 117(1) DGCL (US — written consent in lieu of meeting)]

The undersigned, being all Directors of [COMPANY NAME] entitled to vote on the resolution, resolve as follows:

**Resolution [NUMBER]**

THAT [DESCRIPTION OF RESOLUTION, e.g., "the Company approve and enter into the [Agreement Name] between the Company and [Counterparty Name] on the terms of the draft annexed to this resolution"].

**Resolution [NUMBER]**

THAT [DESCRIPTION OF FURTHER RESOLUTION, e.g., "any one Director be authorized to execute the Agreement and all documents necessary to give effect to it on behalf of the Company"].

[REPEAT FOR ADDITIONAL RESOLUTIONS]

This resolution takes effect on the date on which the last Director signs.

## Template 4: Minutes of Meeting

### Purpose

Minutes record the proceedings and decisions of board meetings and general meetings. They serve as evidence of the decisions made, the matters discussed, and the presence of a quorum. In Australia, a company must keep minutes of meetings and make them available for inspection (Corporations Act 2001 ss 251A, 251AA).

### Template Text — Board Meeting

---

**[COMPANY NAME] [PTY LTD / LIMITED]**
**ACN [COMPANY ACN]**

**MINUTES OF MEETING OF DIRECTORS**

**Date:** [DATE]
**Time:** [TIME]
**Place:** [LOCATION / "By video conference"]

**Present:**
- [DIRECTOR 1 NAME] (Chairperson)
- [DIRECTOR 2 NAME]
- [DIRECTOR 3 NAME]

**In Attendance:**
- [COMPANY SECRETARY NAME] (Company Secretary)
- [OTHER ATTENDEES, e.g., "External Auditor — [NAME] of [FIRM]"]

**Apologies:**
- [DIRECTOR NAME] (apology received and noted)

**1. Opening**

The Chairperson declared a quorum present and opened the meeting at [TIME].

**2. Confirmation of Previous Minutes**

The minutes of the meeting of Directors held on [DATE OF PREVIOUS MEETING] were confirmed as a true and correct record.

*Resolution:* The Board resolved to confirm the minutes of the meeting held on [DATE].
*Moved:* [DIRECTOR NAME] *Seconded:* [DIRECTOR NAME] *Carried unanimously.*

**3. Declaration of Interests**

[DIRECTOR NAME] declared [an interest / no interest] in relation to [MATTER] and [remained for / withdrew from] discussion of that matter. [NOTE: s 191 Corporations Act 2001 — material personal interest disclosure.]

**4. [AGENDA ITEM — e.g., "Financial Report"]**

The [Company Secretary / CFO] presented the financial report for the period ending [DATE].

The Board noted [KEY MATTERS DISCUSSED].

*Resolution:* The Board resolved to [DECISION, e.g., "receive and note the financial report for the period ending [DATE]"].
*Moved:* [DIRECTOR NAME] *Seconded:* [DIRECTOR NAME] *Carried [unanimously / by majority].*

**5. [AGENDA ITEM — e.g., "Approval of Contract"]**

The Board considered the proposed [CONTRACT DESCRIPTION] between the Company and [COUNTERPARTY].

[SUMMARY OF DISCUSSION, KEY POINTS RAISED.]

*Resolution:* The Board resolved to [DECISION].
*Moved:* [DIRECTOR NAME] *Seconded:* [DIRECTOR NAME] *Carried.*

**6. [FURTHER AGENDA ITEMS]**

[CONTINUE AS REQUIRED.]

**7. Next Meeting**

The next meeting of Directors was scheduled for [DATE, TIME, LOCATION].

**8. Close**

There being no further business, the Chairperson declared the meeting closed at [TIME].

---

**Confirmed as a true and correct record:**

Chairperson: ________________________
Name: [CHAIRPERSON NAME]
Date: [DATE OF CONFIRMATION]

### ASIC and Equivalent Filing Requirements

| Document | AU (ASIC) | US (Delaware) | UK (Companies House) | CA (Corporations Canada) | NZ (Companies Office) |
|----------|-----------|---------------|---------------------|------------------------|-----------------------|
| Constitution/Articles | Lodge on adoption or amendment (s 136(5)) | File certificate of incorporation; bylaws not filed | File articles on incorporation; file special resolutions amending articles (s 30) | File articles on incorporation; file by-law amendments | File constitution on incorporation (s 32) |
| Shareholder agreement | Not filed | Not filed | Not filed | Not filed | Not filed |

| Minutes | Not filed (but must be kept for 7 years: s 251A) | Not filed (but must be kept) | Not filed (but must be kept for 10 years: s 248) | Not filed (but must be kept for 7 years) | Not filed (but must be kept) |
