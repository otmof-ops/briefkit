## Overview

This document provides templates for property transactions: contract for sale of land, residential tenancy agreement, commercial lease heads of terms, mortgage/security agreement, and easement grant. Property transactions are among the most heavily regulated areas of legal practice, with jurisdiction-specific requirements for title registration, cooling-off periods, vendor disclosure, and stamp duty/transfer tax.

The fundamental distinction in property law across the covered jurisdictions is between Torrens title (a system of title by registration used in Australia, New Zealand, and parts of Canada) and general law title or recording systems (used in England and Wales, the United States, and parts of Canada). Under Torrens title, registration creates title and the registered proprietor's title is indefeasible (subject to limited exceptions). Under recording systems, deeds create title and recording provides notice but does not cure defects in the underlying title.

## Template 1: Contract for Sale of Land (Residential)

### Purpose

A contract for sale of land is the agreement by which the vendor agrees to sell, and the purchaser agrees to buy, an interest in real property. In most Australian jurisdictions, the sale of residential property must use a prescribed or approved form of contract. In other jurisdictions, the form is more flexible but must comply with the Statute of Frauds requirement that contracts for the sale of land be in writing and signed by the party to be charged.

### Jurisdiction-Specific Requirements

| Requirement | AU (NSW) | US (General) | UK | CA (ON) | NZ |
|-------------|----------|------------|----|---------|----|
| Prescribed form | Standard form prescribed by law societies | No prescribed form; local customs | No prescribed form; Law Society Standard Conditions common | Standard form (OREA Agreement of Purchase and Sale) | Standard form (ADLS/REINZ Agreement) |
| Writing requirement | Conveyancing Act 1919 s 54A | Statute of Frauds (varies by state) | Law of Property (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1989 s 2 | Statute of Frauds (RSO 1990, c S.19) | Property Law Act 2007 s 24 |
| Cooling-off period | 5 business days (Conveyancing Act 1919 s 66S); 0.25% penalty to rescind | Varies; none in many states | None (but survey/search conditions common) | None (conditional period common in practice) | None (but due diligence clause standard in ADLS form) |
| Vendor disclosure | Section 149 certificate, title search, drainage diagram, strata records (NSW) | Varies by state; seller's disclosure form common | Property Information Form (TA6), Fittings and Contents Form (TA10) | Seller Property Information Statement | LIM report, title search |
| Title system | Torrens (Real Property Act 1900) | Recording system (varies by state) | Land Registration Act 2002 (registered land); unregistered land still exists | Land Titles Act (Torrens in ON) or Registry Act | Torrens (Land Transfer Act 2017) |

### Template Text

---

**CONTRACT FOR THE SALE OF LAND**

**Date:** [DATE]

**Vendor:** [VENDOR FULL LEGAL NAME] of [VENDOR ADDRESS]

**Purchaser:** [PURCHASER FULL LEGAL NAME] of [PURCHASER ADDRESS]

**Property:** [FULL PROPERTY ADDRESS]
Being the land comprised in [CERTIFICATE OF TITLE / FOLIO IDENTIFIER / TITLE REFERENCE]: [TITLE REFERENCE NUMBER]
Being Lot [LOT NUMBER] in [DEPOSITED PLAN / STRATA PLAN NUMBER]

**Purchase Price:** $[AMOUNT]

**Deposit:** $[AMOUNT, e.g., "10% of the Purchase Price"], payable as follows:
- $[INITIAL DEPOSIT AMOUNT] on exchange of this Contract; and
- $[BALANCE DEPOSIT] on or before [DATE].

The deposit must be paid to [DEPOSIT HOLDER, e.g., "the Vendor's solicitor"] to be held [as stakeholder / as agent for the Vendor] pending completion.

**Completion Date:** [DATE, OR "forty-two (42) days after the date of this Contract"]

**Inclusions:** [ITEMS INCLUDED IN SALE, e.g., "all fixtures, fittings, curtains, blinds, light fittings, dishwasher, built-in wardrobes"]

**Exclusions:** [ITEMS EXCLUDED FROM SALE]

**Encumbrances:** The property is sold subject to:
(a) [EXISTING ENCUMBRANCES, e.g., "Easement for drainage in favor of [COUNCIL NAME] registered as Dealing No. [NUMBER]"];
(b) [FURTHER ENCUMBRANCES]; and
(c) all existing easements, restrictions, and interests recorded on the Certificate of Title.

---

**SPECIAL CONDITIONS**

**SC1. Finance Condition** [IF APPLICABLE]

SC1.1 This Contract is conditional upon the Purchaser obtaining unconditional approval for a loan of not less than $[LOAN AMOUNT] from a recognized financial institution on terms acceptable to the Purchaser, by [FINANCE DATE].

SC1.2 The Purchaser must use best endeavors to obtain the loan and must notify the Vendor immediately upon receiving approval or being refused.

SC1.3 If the Purchaser does not obtain loan approval by the Finance Date:
(a) the Purchaser may rescind this Contract by written notice to the Vendor given within [NUMBER] business days after the Finance Date;
(b) upon rescission, the deposit must be refunded to the Purchaser in full; and
(c) neither party has any further claim against the other under this Contract.

**SC2. Building and Pest Inspection** [IF APPLICABLE]

SC2.1 This Contract is conditional upon the Purchaser obtaining and being satisfied with a building and pest inspection report on the Property by [INSPECTION DATE].

SC2.2 If the Purchaser is not satisfied with the report, the Purchaser may rescind this Contract by written notice to the Vendor given within [NUMBER] business days after the Inspection Date, and the deposit must be refunded.

**SC3. Strata Records** [IF APPLICABLE — STRATA/UNIT TITLE]

SC3.1 The Vendor must provide the Purchaser with a strata inspection report under [Strata Schemes Management Act 2015 (NSW) s 184 / equivalent legislation] within [NUMBER] days of the date of this Contract.

---

**STANDARD CONDITIONS**

[NOTE: In practice, the standard conditions are those published by the relevant law society or real estate institute. In NSW, the standard conditions are the 2019 Edition of the Contract for the Sale of Land published by the Law Society of NSW and the Real Estate Institute of NSW. The following are summary provisions only — the full standard conditions should be incorporated by reference.]

**1. Completion**

1.1 Completion takes place on the Completion Date at the office of [the Vendor's solicitor / the land titles office / as agreed].

1.2 On completion, the Vendor must:
(a) deliver to the Purchaser a registrable transfer in the approved form;
(b) deliver all keys, access devices, and security codes for the Property; and
(c) ensure the Property is in substantially the same condition as at the date of this Contract (fair wear and tear excepted).

1.3 On completion, the Purchaser must pay the balance of the Purchase Price (less the deposit).

**2. Adjustments**

2.1 The following are adjusted between the parties as at the Completion Date:
(a) council rates and water rates;
(b) strata levies (if applicable);
(c) land tax (if applicable and as provided by the standard conditions); and
(d) rent (if the property is tenanted).

**3. Risk**

3.1 Risk in the Property passes to the Purchaser on [completion / exchange — jurisdiction dependent].

3.2 [NSW: Risk passes on exchange under the standard conditions, but the vendor must maintain insurance until completion. UK: Risk passes on exchange. US: Varies — equitable conversion doctrine or as specified in contract.]

**4. Default**

4.1 If the Purchaser fails to complete on the Completion Date, the Vendor may serve a notice to complete giving the Purchaser [NUMBER, typically 14] days to complete.

4.2 If the Purchaser fails to complete within the notice period, the Vendor may:
(a) terminate the Contract;
(b) forfeit the deposit;
(c) resell the Property; and
(d) claim damages from the Purchaser for any deficiency on resale.

4.3 If the Vendor fails to complete, the Purchaser may serve a notice to complete and, if completion does not occur, may:
(a) terminate the Contract;
(b) recover the deposit; and
(c) claim damages.

---

**SIGNATURES**

Vendor: ________________________
Name: [VENDOR NAME]
Date: [DATE]

Purchaser: ________________________
Name: [PURCHASER NAME]
Date: [DATE]

## Template 3: Commercial Lease — Heads of Terms

### Purpose

Heads of terms (also called letter of intent or term sheet) outline the principal commercial terms of a proposed lease before detailed drafting commences. They are typically expressed to be non-binding (except for confidentiality and costs provisions) and serve as instructions for the solicitors who will prepare the formal lease.

### Template Text

---

**HEADS OF TERMS — COMMERCIAL LEASE**

**Status: Subject to Contract — Not Legally Binding**
(Except clauses [X] (Confidentiality) and [Y] (Costs) which are binding)

**Date:** [DATE]

**Landlord:** [LANDLORD NAME]
**Tenant:** [TENANT NAME]

**Premises:** [ADDRESS, FLOOR, SUITE, APPROXIMATE AREA IN SQ M / SQ FT]

| Term | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Lease Term** | [NUMBER] years |
| **Option to Renew** | [NUMBER] further term(s) of [NUMBER] years each |
| **Commencement Date** | [DATE] |
| **Base Rent** | $[AMOUNT] per annum (exclusive of GST/VAT), payable monthly in advance |
| **Rent Reviews** | [FIXED PERCENTAGE / CPI / MARKET REVIEW] every [NUMBER] years |
| **Outgoings** | Tenant to pay [PROPORTION, e.g., "tenant's proportion of building outgoings"] |
| **Permitted Use** | [PERMITTED USE, e.g., "general office purposes"] |
| **Fitout** | [LANDLORD CONTRIBUTION, e.g., "$[AMOUNT] landlord fitout contribution"] |
| **Incentive** | [RENT-FREE PERIOD, e.g., "[NUMBER] months rent-free"] |
| **Make Good** | Tenant to make good on expiry to [CONDITION, e.g., "base building condition"] |
| **Security** | [BANK GUARANTEE / BOND] for [NUMBER] months' rent and outgoings |
| **Assignment/Subletting** | Subject to landlord's consent, not to be unreasonably withheld |
| **Insurance** | Tenant to maintain public liability ($[AMOUNT]), plate glass, and contents insurance |
| **Governing Law** | [JURISDICTION] |

**Confidentiality.** The parties agree to keep the terms of this document confidential.

**Costs.** Each party bears its own costs of negotiating and documenting the lease.

## Template 4: Mortgage/Security Agreement

### Purpose

A mortgage is a security interest granted over real property to secure repayment of a loan or performance of an obligation. Under Torrens title, a mortgage is created by registration on the title. Under general law, a mortgage historically involved a conveyance of the legal estate with a proviso for reconveyance upon repayment (equity of redemption).

### Template Text (Summary Form)

---

**MORTGAGE**

**Date:** [DATE]

**Mortgagor:** [MORTGAGOR FULL LEGAL NAME] of [ADDRESS] (**"Mortgagor"**)

**Mortgagee:** [MORTGAGEE/LENDER FULL LEGAL NAME] of [ADDRESS] (**"Mortgagee"**)

**Property:** [PROPERTY DESCRIPTION AND TITLE REFERENCE]

**Secured Amount:** [PRINCIPAL AMOUNT] together with interest at [INTEREST RATE] per annum, calculated and charged [FREQUENCY, e.g., "daily and debited monthly"].

**1. Grant of Mortgage**

1.1 In consideration of the Mortgagee advancing or agreeing to advance the Secured Amount to the Mortgagor, the Mortgagor mortgages the Property to the Mortgagee as security for the payment of the Secured Money and the performance of the Mortgagor's obligations under this Mortgage and the associated loan agreement dated [DATE] (the **"Loan Agreement"**).

**2. Mortgagor's Covenants**

The Mortgagor covenants with the Mortgagee to:

(a) pay the Secured Money in accordance with the Loan Agreement;
(b) keep the Property in good repair;
(c) insure the Property for its full replacement value with an insurer approved by the Mortgagee, and note the Mortgagee's interest on the policy;
(d) pay all rates, taxes, and charges on the Property as they fall due;
(e) not lease, transfer, or further encumber the Property without the Mortgagee's prior written consent;
(f) comply with all laws applicable to the Property; and
(g) permit the Mortgagee to inspect the Property on reasonable notice.

**3. Events of Default**

3.1 An Event of Default occurs if:
(a) the Mortgagor fails to pay any amount due under the Loan Agreement within [GRACE PERIOD, e.g., "fourteen (14) days"] of the due date;
(b) the Mortgagor breaches any other obligation under this Mortgage or the Loan Agreement and fails to remedy the breach within [CURE PERIOD, e.g., "thirty (30) days"] of receiving notice;
(c) the Mortgagor becomes insolvent or bankrupt;
(d) the Property is materially damaged and not insured or the insurance proceeds are insufficient; or
(e) a representation or warranty made by the Mortgagor proves to be materially incorrect.

**4. Mortgagee's Rights on Default**

4.1 On the occurrence of an Event of Default, the Mortgagee may:
(a) declare the entire Secured Amount immediately due and payable;
(b) take possession of the Property;
(c) sell the Property by public auction or private treaty [subject to applicable statutory notice requirements]; and
(d) exercise all rights and powers conferred by the [Real Property Act 1900 (NSW) / equivalent legislation] and at law or in equity.

4.2 [AU: The Mortgagee must comply with the notice requirements in section 57(2)(b) of the Real Property Act 1900 (NSW) (one month's notice) before exercising the power of sale. Similar notice requirements exist in all states.]

**5. Application of Sale Proceeds**

5.1 The proceeds of any sale by the Mortgagee must be applied in the following order:
(a) costs and expenses of the sale;
(b) amounts owing to prior encumbrancers (if any);
(c) the Secured Money; and
(d) the surplus (if any) to the Mortgagor.

**6. Registration**

6.1 The Mortgagor must do all things necessary to register this Mortgage on the Certificate of Title for the Property. [AU: The mortgage is registered with the relevant land titles office using the approved form (e.g., Form 05M NSW).]

## Template 5: Easement Grant

### Purpose

An easement is a right held by one landowner (the dominant tenement) to use the land of another (the servient tenement) for a specific purpose. Common easements include rights of way, drainage easements, services easements (for pipes, cables, and utilities), and easements for support. An easement must be created by deed (or by operation of law) and, to bind subsequent owners, must be registered on the title.

### Template Text

---

**EASEMENT**

**Date:** [DATE]

**Grantor:** [SERVIENT TENEMENT OWNER NAME] of [ADDRESS], being the registered proprietor of the land comprised in [TITLE REFERENCE — SERVIENT LOT] (**"Grantor"**)

**Grantee:** [DOMINANT TENEMENT OWNER NAME] of [ADDRESS], being the registered proprietor of the land comprised in [TITLE REFERENCE — DOMINANT LOT] (**"Grantee"**)

**1. Grant of Easement**

1.1 The Grantor grants to the Grantee, as appurtenant to [DOMINANT LOT DESCRIPTION], an easement of [TYPE OF EASEMENT, e.g., "right of carriageway" / "drainage" / "services"] over the land shown marked [REFERENCE, e.g., "[HATCHED/SHADED] on the plan annexed hereto"] on the Servient Land, being part of [SERVIENT LOT DESCRIPTION] (the **"Easement Site"**).

**2. Purpose and Scope**

2.1 The Grantee and all persons authorized by the Grantee may use the Easement Site for the purpose of [DESCRIPTION OF PURPOSE, e.g., "passing and repassing on foot and with vehicles to and from [DOMINANT LOT] and any public road"].

2.2 The Grantee may [ADDITIONAL RIGHTS, e.g., "install, maintain, repair, and replace [DESCRIPTION OF SERVICES, e.g., 'pipes, conduits, and cables'] within the Easement Site for the purpose of providing [SERVICES, e.g., 'water, sewerage, and electricity'] to the Dominant Land"].

**3. Conditions**

3.1 The Grantee must:
(a) exercise the rights granted by this easement in a manner that minimizes interference with the Grantor's use and enjoyment of the Servient Land;
(b) promptly repair any damage to the Servient Land caused by the exercise of the easement;
(c) [IF APPLICABLE: maintain any constructed path, road, or infrastructure within the Easement Site in good repair]; and
(d) not obstruct or interfere with the Grantor's use of the Servient Land except to the extent reasonably necessary to exercise the rights granted by this easement.

3.2 The Grantor must not:
(a) obstruct or interfere with the Grantee's exercise of the easement; or
(b) erect any structure or plant any tree on the Easement Site that would impede the Grantee's exercise of the easement.

**4. Registration**

4.1 The parties must take all steps necessary to register this easement on the Certificates of Title for both the Dominant Land and the Servient Land. The costs of registration are borne by [the Grantee / the parties equally].

**5. Governing Law**

5.1 This easement is governed by the laws of [GOVERNING JURISDICTION].

---

**Executed as a deed.**

[EXECUTION BLOCKS FOR GRANTOR AND GRANTEE]

### Common Pitfalls in Property Templates

1. **Failure to search title before exchange.** The title search reveals encumbrances, caveats, and restrictions that may affect the purchaser's use of the property. Searching after exchange may reveal defects that cannot be resolved before completion.

2. **Inadequate disclosure.** Vendor disclosure obligations are statutory in many jurisdictions. Failure to disclose required information may give the purchaser a right to rescind or claim damages.

3. **Incorrect title reference.** Errors in the folio identifier, lot number, or plan number can invalidate the registration of a transfer, mortgage, or easement.

4. **Missing GST/tax analysis.** The sale of commercial property may be subject to GST/VAT. Residential property may be exempt or subject to special rules (margin scheme in Australia). Failure to address GST in the contract can create significant unexpected liabilities.

5. **Easement too vague.** An easement must be sufficiently defined in terms of location, purpose, and scope. A vague easement may be unenforceable or may be interpreted more broadly or narrowly than the parties intended.
