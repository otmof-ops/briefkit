## Overview

This document provides templates for estate planning instruments: last will and testament, enduring/lasting power of attorney, advance care directive (living will), and discretionary trust deed (family trust). Estate planning documents are among the most technically demanding in legal practice because formal validity requirements are strictly enforced — a will that does not comply with execution requirements is invalid regardless of how clearly it expresses the testator's intentions.

Each jurisdiction has distinct requirements for execution, witnessing, capacity, and revocation. The terminology also differs significantly: what is called an "enduring power of attorney" in Australia is a "lasting power of attorney" in the UK and a "durable power of attorney" in the US. These differences are identified throughout.

**Critical Warning:** Estate planning documents have profound personal and financial consequences. These templates are educational references, not substitutes for professional legal advice. The validity of any estate planning document depends on compliance with the specific requirements of the jurisdiction in which it is intended to operate.

## Template 1: Last Will and Testament

### Purpose

A will is a legal document by which a person (the testator or testatrix) directs the distribution of their estate after death. It typically appoints an executor (personal representative) to administer the estate, names beneficiaries, provides for guardianship of minor children, and may create testamentary trusts.

### Execution Requirements

| Requirement | AU (NSW) | US (General) | UK | CA (ON) | NZ |
|-------------|----------|------------|----|---------|----|
| Governing legislation | Succession Act 2006 (NSW) | Varies by state; UPC adopted in ~18 states | Wills Act 1837 | Succession Law Reform Act (RSO 1990, c S.26) | Wills Act 2007 |
| Age | 18 years (exceptions for married minors, court order) | 18 in most states (some allow 14-16) | 18 (exception for military) | 18 (exception for military, married) | 18 |
| Writing | Required (s 6) | Required | Required (s 9) | Required (s 3) | Required (s 11) |
| Signature | Testator must sign or direct signing (s 6(1)(a)) | Testator must sign | Testator must sign or acknowledge (s 9(a)) | Testator must sign (s 4(1)) | Testator must sign or direct (s 11(a)) |
| Witnesses | 2 witnesses who attest and sign (s 6(1)(b)-(c)) | 2 witnesses (most states) | 2 witnesses (s 9(c)-(d)) | 2 witnesses (s 4(2)) | 2 witnesses (s 11(c)) |
| Witness restrictions | Cannot be beneficiary or spouse of beneficiary | Varies; interested witness rule in some states | Gift to witness or spouse is void (s 15) | Interested witness: gift void (s 12) | Gift to witness void (s 13) |
| Capacity | Must understand nature of will, extent of estate, claims of potential beneficiaries (*Banks v Goodfellow* (1870)) | Same common law test | Same common law test | Same common law test | Same common law test |

### Template Text

---

**LAST WILL AND TESTAMENT**

**of**

**[TESTATOR FULL LEGAL NAME]**

I, [TESTATOR FULL LEGAL NAME], of [TESTATOR ADDRESS], declare that this is my Last Will and Testament and that I revoke all former wills and codicils made by me.

**1. Executor and Trustee**

1.1 I appoint [EXECUTOR 1 FULL NAME] of [ADDRESS] as the Executor and Trustee of this Will.

1.2 If [EXECUTOR 1] is unable or unwilling to act, I appoint [EXECUTOR 2 FULL NAME] of [ADDRESS] as the substitute Executor and Trustee.

1.3 I grant my Executor full power to do all things necessary or desirable to administer my estate, including but not limited to the power to:
(a) sell, lease, mortgage, or otherwise deal with any asset of my estate;
(b) invest funds of my estate in any investment that my Executor considers appropriate;
(c) carry on any business in which I have an interest at the date of my death for such period as my Executor considers appropriate;
(d) compromise, settle, or abandon any claim by or against my estate; and
(e) engage and remunerate professional advisors.

**2. Funeral Wishes**

2.1 I direct that my remains be [BURIAL/CREMATION PREFERENCE]. [NOTE: Funeral directions in a will are expressions of wish only and are not legally binding. They should also be communicated to family members separately, as the will may not be read before the funeral.]

**3. Specific Gifts**

3.1 I give the following specific gifts:

(a) To [BENEFICIARY 1 FULL NAME] of [ADDRESS], [DESCRIPTION OF SPECIFIC GIFT, e.g., "my engagement ring described as [DESCRIPTION]"];

(b) To [BENEFICIARY 2 FULL NAME] of [ADDRESS], the sum of $[AMOUNT];

(c) To [CHARITY NAME] ([ABN/CHARITY NUMBER]), the sum of $[AMOUNT] [or [PERCENTAGE]% of my estate].

3.2 If any beneficiary named in this clause predeceases me, the gift to that beneficiary lapses and falls into the residue of my estate, unless I have specified an alternative beneficiary.

**4. Residuary Estate**

4.1 I give the whole of the residue of my estate (being all property of any kind that I own or am entitled to at the date of my death, after payment of my debts, funeral and testamentary expenses, and the specific gifts in clause 3) to the following persons in the following shares:

(a) [PERCENTAGE]% to [RESIDUARY BENEFICIARY 1 FULL NAME];
(b) [PERCENTAGE]% to [RESIDUARY BENEFICIARY 2 FULL NAME].

4.2 If any residuary beneficiary predeceases me, that beneficiary's share [passes to their children who survive me in equal shares per stirpes / accrues to the surviving residuary beneficiaries in proportion to their respective shares].

**5. Guardian of Minor Children** [IF APPLICABLE]

5.1 If at the date of my death I have any children under the age of 18 years, I appoint [GUARDIAN FULL NAME] of [ADDRESS] as the guardian of those children.

5.2 If [GUARDIAN] is unable or unwilling to act as guardian, I appoint [SUBSTITUTE GUARDIAN FULL NAME] of [ADDRESS] as substitute guardian.

5.3 [NOTE: Appointment of a guardian is effective only if the testator is the last surviving parent with parental responsibility. The Family Court retains overriding jurisdiction over guardianship in the best interests of the child.]

**6. Testamentary Trust for Minor Beneficiaries** [IF APPLICABLE]

6.1 If any beneficiary under this Will is under the age of [VESTING AGE, e.g., "25 years"] at the date of my death, my Executor must hold that beneficiary's share on trust until the beneficiary attains the Vesting Age (the **"Testamentary Trust"**).

6.2 During the Trust Period, my Executor may in their absolute discretion:
(a) apply income and capital of the trust fund for the maintenance, education, advancement, or benefit of the beneficiary; and
(b) invest the trust fund in any investment that my Executor considers appropriate.

6.3 If the beneficiary dies before attaining the Vesting Age, the trust fund is to be distributed [to the beneficiary's children in equal shares / to the residuary beneficiaries / as specified].

**7. General Provisions**

7.1 **Debts and Expenses.** My Executor must pay my legally enforceable debts, funeral expenses, and testamentary expenses from the residue of my estate before distribution.

7.2 **No Contest Clause.** [OPTIONAL — enforceability varies by jurisdiction:] If any beneficiary under this Will directly or indirectly contests the validity of this Will or any of its provisions, the gift to that beneficiary is revoked and that beneficiary's share falls into the residue.

7.3 **Survivorship Period.** A beneficiary must survive me by [SURVIVORSHIP PERIOD, e.g., "thirty (30) days"] to be entitled to receive a gift under this Will. If a beneficiary does not survive me by that period, they are deemed to have predeceased me.

7.4 **Definitions.** In this Will, "children" includes biological and legally adopted children. "Per stirpes" means that if a beneficiary predeceases me, that beneficiary's share passes equally to that beneficiary's children.

---

**ATTESTATION CLAUSE**

Signed by the Testator, [TESTATOR FULL LEGAL NAME], as their Last Will and Testament in the presence of us, both being present at the same time, who at the Testator's request and in the Testator's presence and in the presence of each other have signed our names as witnesses:

Testator's Signature: ________________________
Date: [DATE]

**Witness 1:**
Signature: ________________________
Full Name: [WITNESS 1 NAME]
Address: [WITNESS 1 ADDRESS]
Occupation: [WITNESS 1 OCCUPATION]

**Witness 2:**
Signature: ________________________
Full Name: [WITNESS 2 NAME]
Address: [WITNESS 2 ADDRESS]
Occupation: [WITNESS 2 OCCUPATION]

### Revocation

| Method | Requirements | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Later will with revocation clause | Properly executed later will stating revocation of all earlier wills | Most common and reliable method |
| Codicil | Properly executed codicil revoking specific provisions | Used for minor amendments only |
| Physical destruction | Testator destroys will with intention to revoke | Must be by testator or at testator's direction; risk of disputes about intent |
| Marriage | Marriage automatically revokes will in most jurisdictions | AU: s 12 Succession Act 2006 (NSW); UK: s 18 Wills Act 1837. Exceptions for wills made in contemplation of marriage |
| Divorce | Gifts to former spouse revoked in some jurisdictions | AU: s 13 Succession Act 2006 (NSW); varies by state in US |

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Interested witness.** A gift to a witness (or the witness's spouse/partner) is void in most jurisdictions. Always use independent witnesses.
2. **Failure to address digital assets.** Modern wills should address digital assets including online accounts, cryptocurrency, and digital media libraries.
3. **No residuary clause.** Without a residuary clause, assets not specifically gifted pass on intestacy — potentially to different people than the testator intended.
4. **Assuming joint assets pass under the will.** Assets held as joint tenants pass by survivorship, not under the will. The will only governs assets held as tenants in common or solely.

## Template 2: Enduring/Lasting Power of Attorney (Financial)

### Purpose

An enduring power of attorney (EPA) (called a lasting power of attorney (LPA) in the UK, or a durable power of attorney in the US) authorizes a nominated person (the attorney or agent) to make financial and legal decisions on behalf of the principal. Unlike an ordinary power of attorney, it continues to operate (or commences operation) if the principal loses mental capacity.

### Terminology by Jurisdiction

| Jurisdiction | Instrument Name | Governing Legislation |
|-------------|----------------|----------------------|
| NSW | Enduring Power of Attorney | Powers of Attorney Act 2003 (NSW) |
| VIC | Enduring Power of Attorney | Powers of Attorney Act 2014 (Vic) |
| QLD | Enduring Power of Attorney | Powers of Attorney Act 1998 (Qld) |
| US | Durable Power of Attorney | Varies by state; Uniform Power of Attorney Act adopted in ~20 states |
| UK (E&W) | Lasting Power of Attorney (Property and Financial Affairs) | Mental Capacity Act 2005 |
| CA (ON) | Continuing Power of Attorney for Property | Substitute Decisions Act, 1992 (ON) |
| NZ | Enduring Power of Attorney (property) | Protection of Personal and Property Rights Act 1988 |

### Template Text

---

**ENDURING POWER OF ATTORNEY**
**(Financial / Property Matters)**

[NOTE: Most Australian states have prescribed forms that must be used. This template follows the general structure; the prescribed form for the relevant state must be used for a valid instrument.]

**1. Principal**

I, [PRINCIPAL FULL LEGAL NAME], of [PRINCIPAL ADDRESS], date of birth [DATE OF BIRTH], appoint the following person(s) as my attorney(s).

**2. Attorney(s)**

I appoint:

(a) [ATTORNEY 1 FULL NAME] of [ADDRESS], date of birth [DATE OF BIRTH]; and

(b) [ATTORNEY 2 FULL NAME] of [ADDRESS], date of birth [DATE OF BIRTH],

[SELECT ONE:]
- jointly (both must agree on every decision);
- severally (either may act independently); or
- jointly and severally (may act together or independently).

**3. Substitute Attorney** [IF APPLICABLE]

If any of the above attorneys is unable or unwilling to act, I appoint [SUBSTITUTE ATTORNEY FULL NAME] of [ADDRESS] as substitute attorney.

**4. Commencement**

This Power of Attorney [SELECT ONE]:
(a) commences immediately and continues even if I lose decision-making capacity (**"enduring"**); or
(b) commences only when I lose decision-making capacity (**"springing"**).

**5. Scope of Authority**

My attorney(s) may do on my behalf anything that I could lawfully do by an attorney, including but not limited to:
(a) operate my bank accounts;
(b) pay my debts and expenses;
(c) manage my investments;
(d) deal with my real and personal property, including the power to sell, lease, mortgage, or otherwise deal with that property;
(e) conduct my business affairs;
(f) make gifts on my behalf [OPTIONAL: subject to a limit of $[AMOUNT] per recipient per year / limited to customary gifts at holidays and birthdays]; and
(g) engage and pay professional advisors.

**6. Conditions and Limitations** [IF APPLICABLE]

[SPECIFY ANY CONDITIONS OR LIMITATIONS, e.g.:]
(a) My attorney must not sell my residential property at [ADDRESS] without the consent of [SPECIFIED PERSON].
(b) My attorney must not make gifts exceeding $[AMOUNT] per year in total.
(c) My attorney must keep records of all transactions and provide an accounting to [SPECIFIED PERSON] annually.

**7. Enduring Nature**

I intend this Power of Attorney to continue to be effective even if I subsequently lose my decision-making capacity. [NOTE: This clause is essential for the power to be "enduring" in AU, "lasting" in UK, or "durable" in US.]

---

**PRINCIPAL'S SIGNATURE**

Signed by the Principal: ________________________
Name: [PRINCIPAL NAME]
Date: [DATE]

**WITNESS**

[AU: Requirements vary by state. NSW: Prescribed witness must be an eligible witness as defined in the Powers of Attorney Act 2003. UK: LPA must be registered with the Office of the Public Guardian to be valid. US: Requirements vary; notarization commonly required.]

Signature: ________________________
Name: [WITNESS NAME]
Qualification: [QUALIFICATION, e.g., "Australian legal practitioner"]

**ATTORNEY'S ACCEPTANCE**

I, [ATTORNEY NAME], accept this appointment and undertake to act in the Principal's best interests and in accordance with this Power of Attorney and applicable law.

Signature: ________________________
Date: [DATE]

---

## Template 3: Advance Care Directive / Living Will

### Purpose

An advance care directive (also called a living will, advance decision, or advance health directive) records a person's wishes about future medical treatment in the event they lose capacity to make those decisions. It may include refusal of specific treatments, preferences for palliative care, and appointment of a substitute decision-maker for health matters.

### Terminology by Jurisdiction

| Jurisdiction | Instrument Name | Governing Legislation |
|-------------|----------------|----------------------|
| NSW | Advance Care Directive | Not yet formally legislated in NSW; common law validity recognized |
| QLD | Advance Health Directive | Powers of Attorney Act 1998 (Qld) Part 3 |
| VIC | Advance Care Directive | Medical Treatment Planning and Decisions Act 2016 (Vic) |
| SA | Advance Care Directive | Advance Care Directives Act 2013 (SA) |
| US | Advance Directive / Living Will | Varies by state; Patient Self-Determination Act 1990 (federal) |
| UK | Advance Decision to Refuse Treatment | Mental Capacity Act 2005 ss 24-26 |
| CA (ON) | Power of Attorney for Personal Care | Health Care Consent Act, 1996 (ON) |
| NZ | Advance Directive | Common law; Code of Health and Disability Services Consumers' Rights |

### Template Text

---

**ADVANCE CARE DIRECTIVE**

**1. Personal Details**

Full Name: [FULL LEGAL NAME]
Date of Birth: [DATE OF BIRTH]
Address: [ADDRESS]

**2. Statement of Values**

I make this Advance Care Directive to record my wishes and preferences about my future health care if I become unable to make or communicate my own decisions. I have the following values and beliefs that should guide decision-making about my care:

[PERSONAL STATEMENT OF VALUES, e.g., "I value quality of life over length of life. I do not wish to be kept alive by artificial means if there is no reasonable prospect of meaningful recovery. I wish to be kept comfortable and free from pain."]

**3. Substitute Decision-Maker** [WHERE PERMITTED BY LEGISLATION]

I appoint [SUBSTITUTE DECISION-MAKER NAME] of [ADDRESS], relationship: [RELATIONSHIP], as my substitute decision-maker for health and personal care decisions.

If [SUBSTITUTE DECISION-MAKER] is unable or unwilling to act, I appoint [ALTERNATIVE NAME] of [ADDRESS].

**4. Treatment Directions**

If I am in a condition where I am unable to make or communicate decisions about my medical treatment, I direct as follows:

**4.1 Terminal Illness.** If I have a terminal illness with no reasonable prospect of recovery:
(a) I [consent to / refuse] cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR);
(b) I [consent to / refuse] mechanical ventilation (life support);
(c) I [consent to / refuse] artificial nutrition and hydration (tube feeding);
(d) I [consent to] palliative care to maintain comfort and dignity; and
(e) I [consent to] medication for the relief of pain even if it may hasten death.

**4.2 Permanent Unconsciousness.** If I am in a persistent vegetative state or permanent coma with no reasonable prospect of regaining consciousness:
(a) I [consent to / refuse] all life-sustaining treatment; and
(b) I [consent to] comfort care and pain management only.

**4.3 Advanced Dementia.** If I have advanced dementia and am unable to recognize family members, communicate meaningfully, or perform basic self-care:
[SPECIFY TREATMENT PREFERENCES]

**5. Organ and Tissue Donation**

I [wish / do not wish] to donate my organs and tissues for transplantation upon my death. [IF WISH:] I authorize the donation of [all suitable organs and tissues / the following specific organs: [SPECIFY]].

**6. Revocation**

I may revoke or amend this Advance Care Directive at any time while I have capacity to do so, by [making a new directive / destroying this document / notifying my substitute decision-maker in writing].

---

**PRINCIPAL'S SIGNATURE**

Signed: ________________________
Name: [FULL NAME]
Date: [DATE]

**WITNESSES** [Requirements vary by jurisdiction]

Witness 1 Signature: ________________________
Name: [NAME]
[Qualification if required, e.g., "Medical practitioner" in some states]

Witness 2 Signature: ________________________
Name: [NAME]

---

### Key Legal Issues

| Issue | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Binding effect | In the UK, an advance decision to refuse life-sustaining treatment must be in writing, signed, and witnessed (MCA 2005 s 25(5)-(6)). In AU, varies by state. In US, varies by state. |
| Capacity | The principal must have capacity at the time of making the directive. The *Banks v Goodfellow* principles apply by analogy. |
| Specificity | The directive must be sufficiently specific about the treatment being refused and the circumstances in which the refusal applies. Vague directives may not be binding. |
| Medical override | In most jurisdictions, a healthcare provider may decline to follow a directive if they believe the principal did not have capacity, the circumstances have changed materially, or the directive is ambiguous. |

## Template 4: Discretionary Trust Deed (Family Trust)

### Purpose

A discretionary trust (family trust) is a trust structure commonly used for asset protection, income splitting, and estate planning. The trustee has discretion to distribute income and capital among a defined class of beneficiaries. In Australia, family trusts are widely used for tax planning under the Income Tax Assessment Act 1936 and 1997. The trust deed is the constituting document that creates the trust and defines its terms.

### Template Text (Summary)

---

**DEED OF TRUST**
**[TRUST NAME] FAMILY TRUST**

**Date:** [DATE]

**Settlor:** [SETTLOR NAME] of [ADDRESS]

**Trustee:** [TRUSTEE NAME — individual or corporate trustee] of [ADDRESS]

**Settlement Sum:** $[AMOUNT, typically $10-$100]

**1. Establishment of Trust**

1.1 The Settlor settles the Settlement Sum upon the Trustee to hold on the trusts declared in this Deed, and the Trustee accepts the trusts of this Deed.

1.2 The trust established by this Deed is known as the [TRUST NAME] Family Trust.

**2. Definitions**

**"Beneficiary"** means any person or entity within the class of beneficiaries defined in Schedule 1, including:
(a) [PRIMARY BENEFICIARY NAME] (the **"Primary Beneficiary"**);
(b) the spouse, children, grandchildren, and remoter issue of the Primary Beneficiary;
(c) the parents and siblings of the Primary Beneficiary;
(d) any company, trust, or entity controlled by a person referred to in (a)-(c);
(e) any charity; and
(f) any person or entity nominated by the Trustee with the consent of the [Appointor/Guardian].

**"Appointor"** means [APPOINTOR NAME] of [ADDRESS], or their successor appointed in accordance with this Deed.

**"Trust Fund"** means the Settlement Sum and all property from time to time held by the Trustee on the trusts of this Deed.

**"Vesting Date"** means [DATE — typically 80 years from establishment, being within the perpetuity period] or such earlier date as the Trustee determines with the consent of the Appointor.

**3. Trustee's Discretions**

3.1 **Income.** In each financial year, the Trustee may in its absolute discretion:
(a) distribute the whole or any part of the net income of the Trust Fund among such of the Beneficiaries in such proportions as the Trustee determines; or
(b) accumulate the whole or any part of the net income.

3.2 **Capital.** The Trustee may in its absolute discretion distribute the whole or any part of the capital of the Trust Fund to or for the benefit of any one or more of the Beneficiaries.

3.3 No Beneficiary has any entitlement to income or capital unless and until the Trustee exercises its discretion in favor of that Beneficiary.

**4. Trustee's Powers**

4.1 The Trustee has all the powers of a natural person who is the absolute and beneficial owner of the Trust Fund, including the power to:
(a) invest in any investment, whether or not authorized by law for the investment of trust funds;
(b) borrow money and give security over the Trust Fund;
(c) carry on or participate in any business;
(d) acquire, hold, lease, mortgage, sell, or otherwise deal with real and personal property;
(e) lend money to Beneficiaries on such terms as the Trustee considers appropriate;
(f) establish and contribute to superannuation funds for Beneficiaries; and
(g) do all things incidental to the administration of the Trust.

**5. Appointor**

5.1 The Appointor has the power to:
(a) remove the Trustee and appoint a new Trustee; and
(b) consent to the exercise of certain powers specified in this Deed.

5.2 The Appointor may appoint a successor Appointor by deed. If the Appointor dies without appointing a successor, the role passes to [SUCCESSION PROVISION, e.g., "the Primary Beneficiary, or if the Primary Beneficiary is unable or unwilling to act, the eldest adult child of the Primary Beneficiary"].

**6. Trustee's Duties**

6.1 The Trustee must:
(a) act honestly and in good faith in the best interests of the Beneficiaries;
(b) exercise reasonable care, skill, and diligence;
(c) keep proper accounts and records;
(d) provide accounts to any Beneficiary on reasonable request; and
(e) comply with all applicable taxation and regulatory requirements.

**7. Vesting**

7.1 On the Vesting Date, the Trustee must distribute the Trust Fund among the Beneficiaries in such proportions as the Trustee determines. If the Trustee does not make a determination, the Trust Fund is distributed equally among the Beneficiaries then living.

**8. Governing Law**

8.1 This Deed and the trusts declared in it are governed by the laws of [GOVERNING JURISDICTION].

---

**EXECUTION**

Settlor: ________________________
Name: [SETTLOR NAME]
Date: [DATE]

Trustee: ________________________
Name: [TRUSTEE NAME]
Date: [DATE]

---

### Key Issues

| Issue | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Perpetuity period | AU: 80 years (varies by state). UK: 125 years (Perpetuities and Accumulations Act 2009). US: Varies; some states have abolished the rule against perpetuities. |
| Taxation | AU: Trust income distributed to beneficiaries is taxed in their hands. Undistributed income taxed at highest marginal rate (s 99A ITAA 1936). Family trust election may be required for losses. |
| Stamp duty | Some states impose stamp duty on the creation or transfer of trust property. Varies by jurisdiction. |
| Trustee liability | The trustee is personally liable for trust debts, with a right of indemnity from the trust fund. A corporate trustee limits personal exposure of the individuals involved. |
