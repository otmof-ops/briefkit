## Overview

This document provides templates for regulatory compliance documents: privacy policy, data breach notification, freedom of information request, whistleblower report, and modern slavery statement. Regulatory compliance is among the fastest-evolving areas of legal practice, driven by the expansion of data protection frameworks (GDPR, Privacy Act 1988, CCPA), whistleblower protection legislation, and human rights due diligence requirements.

Unlike transactional templates where the parties negotiate terms, regulatory compliance templates must satisfy mandatory statutory content requirements. The template is not a starting point for negotiation — it is a minimum specification that must be met to achieve compliance. Omitting a required element may constitute a breach of the applicable legislation and attract penalties.

## Template 1: Privacy Policy

### Purpose

A privacy policy is a public-facing document that informs individuals about how an organization collects, uses, stores, and discloses personal information. It is required by law in most jurisdictions where an organization handles personal information. The content requirements differ between frameworks but share a common structure: what is collected, why, how it is used, who it is shared with, how it is protected, and what rights individuals have.

### Statutory Requirements by Framework

| Requirement | Privacy Act 1988 (AU) — APP 1.4 | GDPR (EU) — Arts 13-14 | CCPA/CPRA (US-CA) | Privacy Act 2020 (NZ) |
|-------------|----------------------------------|------------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Identity of organization | Required | Required (data controller) | Required | Required |
| Types of information collected | Required | Required (categories) | Required (categories) | Required |
| Purpose of collection | Required | Required (purposes + legal basis) | Required | Required |
| How information is collected | Required | Required | Not explicitly required | Required |
| Third-party disclosure | Required | Required (recipients/categories) | Required (categories sold/shared) | Required |
| Overseas transfer | Required (APP 8) | Required (Art 13(1)(f)) | Not specifically | Required (IPP 12) |
| Access and correction rights | Required | Required (right of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection) | Required (right to know, delete, opt-out, non-discrimination) | Required |
| Complaint process | Required | Required (right to lodge complaint with supervisory authority) | Required | Required |
| Data retention | Required | Required | Required (retention period or criteria) | Required |
| Automated decision-making | Not specifically required | Required if applicable (Art 22) | Required for profiling (CPRA) | Not specifically required |

### Template Text

---

**PRIVACY POLICY**

**[ORGANIZATION NAME]**

**Last Updated:** [DATE]

**1. About This Policy**

[GDPR ADDITION: For the purposes of the GDPR, we are the data controller. Our representative in the EU is [EU REPRESENTATIVE NAME AND ADDRESS]. Our Data Protection Officer can be contacted at [DPO EMAIL].]

## Template 2: Data Breach Notification

### Purpose

A data breach notification is a formal report made to the relevant regulatory authority (and affected individuals) when personal information is subject to unauthorized access, disclosure, loss, or destruction. Mandatory data breach notification is required under the Privacy Act 1988 (AU — Notifiable Data Breaches scheme, Part IIIC), the GDPR (Arts 33-34), and various US state breach notification laws.

### Notification Timeframes

| Jurisdiction | Regulator Notification | Individual Notification |
|-------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| AU | As soon as practicable after becoming aware (s 26WH) | As soon as practicable (s 26WK) |
| EU (GDPR) | 72 hours (Art 33(1)) | Without undue delay (Art 34(1)) |
| UK | 72 hours (UK GDPR Art 33(1)) | Without undue delay |
| US (CA) | Without unreasonable delay, no later than 45 days | Without unreasonable delay (Cal. Civ. Code s 1798.82) |
| CA (Federal) | As soon as feasible (PIPEDA s 10.1(1)) | As soon as feasible |
| NZ | As soon as practicable after becoming aware (Privacy Act 2020 s 114) | As soon as practicable (s 115) |

### Template Text — Notification to OAIC (AU)

---

**NOTIFIABLE DATA BREACH STATEMENT**
**Submitted to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner**

Pursuant to section 26WK of the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)

**1. Organization Details**

Organization Name: [ORGANIZATION NAME]
ABN/ACN: [NUMBER]
Contact Person: [NAME AND TITLE]
Contact Email: [EMAIL]
Contact Phone: [PHONE]

**2. Description of the Breach**

2.1 **Nature of the breach:** [DESCRIPTION, e.g., "Unauthorized access to a database containing customer personal information due to a compromised employee credential."]

2.2 **Date the breach occurred:** [DATE] (or estimated date if exact date unknown)

2.3 **Date the breach was discovered:** [DATE]

2.4 **How the breach was discovered:** [DESCRIPTION]

**3. Information Involved**

3.1 **Types of personal information involved:**
- [ ] Names
- [ ] Email addresses
- [ ] Phone numbers
- [ ] Residential addresses
- [ ] Dates of birth
- [ ] Financial information (bank accounts, credit card numbers)
- [ ] Tax file numbers / Social Security numbers
- [ ] Health information
- [ ] Identity document numbers (passport, driver license)
- [ ] Other: [SPECIFY]

3.2 **Number of individuals affected:** [NUMBER or ESTIMATED RANGE]

**4. Assessment of Serious Harm**

4.1 We have assessed this breach as an "eligible data breach" under section 26WE of the Privacy Act 1988 because [EXPLAIN WHY THE BREACH IS LIKELY TO RESULT IN SERIOUS HARM TO ANY OF THE INDIVIDUALS TO WHOM THE INFORMATION RELATES].

4.2 The following factors were considered in this assessment (per s 26WG):
(a) the kind(s) and sensitivity of the information;
(b) whether the information is protected by security measures and the likelihood that those measures could be overcome;
(c) the persons who have obtained or could obtain the information;
(d) the nature of the harm that could result; and
(e) any other relevant matters.

**5. Remedial Action**

5.1 We have taken the following steps to contain the breach and mitigate harm:
(a) [ACTION 1, e.g., "Disabled the compromised credentials and reset all affected passwords."]
(b) [ACTION 2, e.g., "Engaged a cybersecurity firm to investigate the breach and secure systems."]
(c) [ACTION 3, e.g., "Notified affected individuals by email on [DATE]."]
(d) [ACTION 4]

**6. Notification to Individuals**

6.1 We have notified [NUMBER] affected individuals by [METHOD] on [DATE].

(c) [RECOMMENDATION 3, e.g., "Contact IDCARE (1800 595 160) for identity protection support."]

## Template 3: Freedom of Information Request

### Purpose

A freedom of information (FOI) request is a formal request for access to government-held documents. The right of access to government information is established by the Freedom of Information Act 1982 (Cth) (AU), the Freedom of Information Act 2000 (UK), the Freedom of Information Act 5 USC s 552 (US — FOIA), and equivalent legislation in other jurisdictions.

### Template Text

---

**FREEDOM OF INFORMATION REQUEST**

**To:** [AGENCY FOI OFFICER]
[AGENCY NAME]
[AGENCY ADDRESS]

**From:** [APPLICANT NAME]
[APPLICANT ADDRESS]
[APPLICANT EMAIL]
[APPLICANT PHONE]

**Date:** [DATE]

**Dear FOI Officer,**

I make this request under the [Freedom of Information Act 1982 (Cth) / Freedom of Information Act 2000 (UK) / Freedom of Information Act 5 USC s 552 (US)] for access to the following documents:

**Documents Requested:**

1. [DESCRIPTION OF DOCUMENTS, e.g., "All documents, including correspondence, memoranda, briefing papers, and file notes, relating to [SUBJECT MATTER] created or received by [AGENCY/DEPARTMENT] between [START DATE] and [END DATE]."]

2. [ADDITIONAL CATEGORIES OF DOCUMENTS.]

**Form of Access:**

I request access in the form of [copies of the documents / inspection of the documents / electronic copies in [FORMAT]].

**Application Fee:**

[AU: I enclose the application fee of $[AMOUNT — currently nil for personal information requests; fees may apply for non-personal requests under some jurisdictions].]

[UK: No application fee is payable under the FOIA 2000.]

[US: I am willing to pay fees up to $[AMOUNT]. If the fees will exceed this amount, please contact me before processing the request. I request a fee waiver on the basis that disclosure is in the public interest because [GROUNDS FOR WAIVER].]

**Processing Time:**

[AU: I understand the agency must process this request within 30 days (s 15(5)(b) FOI Act 1982 (Cth)).]

[UK: I understand the authority must respond within 20 working days (s 10 FOIA 2000).]

[US: I understand the agency must respond within 20 business days (5 USC s 552(a)(6)(A)).]

Yours faithfully,

[APPLICANT NAME]
[APPLICANT SIGNATURE]

## Template 4: Whistleblower Report

### Purpose

A whistleblower report is a disclosure of suspected wrongdoing made to an eligible recipient under whistleblower protection legislation. In Australia, the Corporations Act 2001 Part 9.4AAA and the Taxation Administration Act 1953 Part IVA provide protections for whistleblowers who report breaches of corporation or tax law. The Public Interest Disclosure Act 2013 (Cth) covers disclosures about public sector wrongdoing. Equivalent protections exist in other jurisdictions.

### Template Text

---

**CONFIDENTIAL — WHISTLEBLOWER DISCLOSURE**

**To:** [ELIGIBLE RECIPIENT, e.g., "Whistleblower Protection Officer" / "External Auditor" / "ASIC" / "ATO" / "Board Audit Committee Chair"]

**Date:** [DATE]

**1. Discloser Information** [NOTE: Anonymous disclosures are protected under Australian law (s 1317AAA Corporations Act 2001)]

Name: [NAME — or "Anonymous"]
Position: [POSITION — if comfortable disclosing]
Contact: [CONTACT DETAILS — if comfortable disclosing]

**2. Nature of Disclosure**

I have reasonable grounds to suspect that [ENTITY NAME] or persons associated with it have engaged in conduct that constitutes:

- [ ] A contravention of the Corporations Act 2001 or ASIC Act 2001
- [ ] A contravention of a Commonwealth law punishable by imprisonment of 12 months or more
- [ ] Conduct that represents a danger to the public or the financial system
- [ ] A contravention of taxation law
- [ ] Other misconduct or an improper state of affairs: [SPECIFY]

**3. Description of Conduct**

[DETAILED FACTUAL DESCRIPTION OF THE SUSPECTED WRONGDOING, INCLUDING:
- What happened
- When it happened (dates, frequency)
- Who was involved
- Where it occurred
- How you became aware of it
- Any documents or evidence supporting the disclosure]

**4. Supporting Evidence**

I am able to provide the following evidence in support of this disclosure:
(a) [DESCRIPTION OF EVIDENCE, e.g., "Emails between [PERSONS] dated [DATES]"]
(b) [FURTHER EVIDENCE]

**5. Acknowledgment of Protections**

I understand that as a whistleblower I am entitled to protections under [the Corporations Act 2001 Part 9.4AAA / applicable legislation], including:
- Protection of my identity (the recipient must not disclose my identity without my consent or as required by law)
- Protection from detrimental conduct (victimization, dismissal, discrimination)
- Protection from civil, criminal, and administrative liability for making the disclosure
- Compensation and remedies if I suffer detriment as a result of the disclosure

## Template 5: Modern Slavery Statement

### Purpose

A modern slavery statement is a public disclosure required of large organizations under the Modern Slavery Act 2018 (Cth) (AU — entities with consolidated revenue of $100 million or more) and the Modern Slavery Act 2015 (UK — entities with turnover of 36 million pounds or more). The statement must describe the organization's structure, operations, and supply chains, and the actions taken to assess and address modern slavery risks.

### Mandatory Content

| Requirement | AU (Modern Slavery Act 2018 s 16) | UK (Modern Slavery Act 2015 s 54) |
|-------------|-----------------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Organization's structure, operations, supply chains | Required | Required |
| Risks of modern slavery in operations and supply chains | Required | Required |
| Actions taken to assess and address risks | Required | Required |
| How effectiveness is assessed | Required | Required |
| Process of consultation with owned/controlled entities | Required | Not specifically required |
| Approval by principal governing body | Required | Required (board approval + director signature) |
| Other relevant information | Required | Optional |

### Template Text (Summary)

---

**MODERN SLAVERY STATEMENT**
**[FINANCIAL YEAR]**

**[ORGANIZATION NAME]**

This statement is made pursuant to [section 13 of the Modern Slavery Act 2018 (Cth) / section 54 of the Modern Slavery Act 2015 (UK)] for the financial year ending [DATE].

**1. About [ORGANIZATION NAME]**

[DESCRIPTION OF ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE, OPERATIONS, AND SUPPLY CHAINS. Include: legal structure, countries of operation, number of employees, nature of business, key supply chain categories.]

**2. Modern Slavery Risks**

We have identified the following potential modern slavery risks in our operations and supply chains:

(a) [RISK 1, e.g., "Use of migrant labor in [INDUSTRY/COUNTRY] supply chains with limited visibility beyond tier-one suppliers."]
(b) [RISK 2]
(c) [RISK 3]

**3. Actions Taken**

We have taken the following actions to assess and address modern slavery risks:

(a) [ACTION 1, e.g., "Implemented a supplier code of conduct requiring all direct suppliers to comply with applicable labor laws and prohibiting the use of forced, bonded, or child labor."]
(b) [ACTION 2, e.g., "Conducted risk assessments of our supply chain using [METHODOLOGY/TOOL]."]
(c) [ACTION 3, e.g., "Provided training to [NUMBER] employees in procurement, HR, and management on identifying modern slavery indicators."]
(d) [ACTION 4, e.g., "Established a grievance mechanism for workers in our supply chain to report concerns."]

**4. Assessing Effectiveness**

We assess the effectiveness of our actions through:
(a) [METRIC 1, e.g., "Regular supplier audits, including unannounced site visits."]
(b) [METRIC 2, e.g., "Tracking the number of modern slavery risks identified and remediated."]
(c) [METRIC 3, e.g., "Employee training completion rates."]

**5. Consultation**

[AU: We consulted with the following owned or controlled entities in preparing this statement: [LIST ENTITIES AND DESCRIBE CONSULTATION PROCESS].]

**6. Approval**

This statement was approved by the Board of Directors of [ORGANIZATION NAME] on [DATE].

Signed: ________________________
[DIRECTOR/CEO NAME]
[TITLE]
Date: [DATE]
