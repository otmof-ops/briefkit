# Correspondence Templates

**Jurisdiction Coverage:** AU, US, UK, CA, NZ

**Paper section references:** [`accc-compliance-enforcement-policy-2025-26.pdf`](accc-compliance-enforcement-policy-2025-26.pdf), [`accc-guide-competition-consumer-law.pdf`](accc-guide-competition-consumer-law.pdf)

## Overview

This document provides templates for professional legal correspondence: letter of demand, cease and desist letter, engagement letter (retainer), costs agreement/disclosure, without prejudice settlement offer, and notice of termination of retainer. Legal correspondence carries legal effect — a letter of demand may start limitation periods running, a without prejudice letter may be inadmissible in evidence, and an engagement letter creates a contractual relationship with binding obligations.

The professional obligations governing legal correspondence are set out in the applicable solicitors' conduct rules. In Australia, the Australian Solicitors' Conduct Rules 2015 (ASCR) govern. In the US, the ABA Model Rules of Professional Conduct apply (adopted with variations in each state). In the UK, the SRA Code of Conduct for Solicitors applies.

## Template 1: Letter of Demand

### Purpose

A letter of demand is a formal written request for payment or performance, typically sent before commencing legal proceedings. It serves several purposes: it may be required by pre-action protocols (such as the Practice Note CM 5 in the Federal Court of Australia, or the Pre-Action Protocol for Debt Claims in England and Wales); it demonstrates that the creditor has taken reasonable steps to resolve the dispute; and it may trigger the running of interest or costs consequences.

### Legal Effect

| Effect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Pre-action requirement | Many courts require evidence of a demand before proceedings can commence |
| Costs consequences | Failure to respond reasonably to a demand may result in indemnity costs |
| Interest | Some statutes allow interest from the date of demand |
| Limitation | Does not restart limitation period, but may be relevant to discoverability |
| Evidence | Generally admissible as evidence of the claim and the defendant's failure to comply |

### Template Text

---

**[FIRM LETTERHEAD]**

[DATE]

**BY [METHOD: Registered Post / Email / Hand Delivery]**

[RECIPIENT NAME]
[RECIPIENT ADDRESS]

**Dear [RECIPIENT NAME],**

**Re: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF MATTER]**
**Our Ref: [FILE REFERENCE]**

We act for [CLIENT NAME] (**"our client"**) in relation to [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF THE RELATIONSHIP OR TRANSACTION, e.g., "a services agreement dated [DATE] between our client and you/your company"].

**Background**

[SET OUT THE FACTUAL BACKGROUND CONCISELY, e.g.:]

1. On or about [DATE], our client and [RECIPIENT/COMPANY] entered into [AGREEMENT TYPE] pursuant to which [RECIPIENT] agreed to [OBLIGATION].

2. [RECIPIENT] has failed to [SPECIFIC BREACH, e.g., "pay the invoice dated [DATE] in the amount of $[AMOUNT] which was due on [DUE DATE]"].

3. Despite [PRIOR REQUESTS/REMINDERS, e.g., "our client's reminder dated [DATE] and telephone call on [DATE]"], the amount remains outstanding.

**Demand**

We are instructed to demand, and hereby demand, that you:

1. [SPECIFIC DEMAND, e.g., "Pay the sum of $[AMOUNT] (being $[PRINCIPAL] in principal, plus $[INTEREST] in interest calculated at [RATE]% per annum from [DATE] to the date of this letter) within [TIMEFRAME, e.g., 'fourteen (14) days'] of the date of this letter."]

2. [ADDITIONAL DEMANDS IF APPLICABLE, e.g., "Deliver to our client the [PROPERTY/DOCUMENTS] as required by clause [X] of the Agreement."]

**Consequences of Non-Compliance**

If you fail to comply with this demand within the time specified, our client will commence legal proceedings against you without further notice. In those proceedings, our client will seek:

(a) the amounts demanded above;
(b) interest pursuant to [APPLICABLE INTEREST PROVISION, e.g., "section 100 of the Civil Procedure Act 2005 (NSW)" / "the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 (UK)"];
(c) legal costs on an indemnity basis; and
(d) any other relief the court considers appropriate.

We draw your attention to the [PRE-ACTION PROTOCOL, e.g., "overriding purpose provisions in section 56 of the Civil Procedure Act 2005 (NSW)" / "Pre-Action Protocol for Debt Claims under the Civil Procedure Rules (UK)"] and note that this letter is relevant to the court's assessment of costs.

**Response**

Please direct all correspondence to the undersigned. We request that you do not contact our client directly in relation to this matter.

If you dispute the claim, please provide full particulars of your dispute in writing within [TIMEFRAME] so that we may consider whether the matter can be resolved without proceedings.

Yours faithfully,

[SOLICITOR NAME]
[FIRM NAME]
[DIRECT PHONE]
[EMAIL]

---

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Demanding more than is owed.** A letter that demands a grossly inflated amount may be considered misleading conduct. Ensure the amount is accurate and supported by documentation.
2. **Threatening criminal proceedings to gain a civil advantage.** This may constitute an offense in some jurisdictions and is an ethical breach under conduct rules (ASCR r 34.1.1).
3. **No time for response.** The demand should allow a reasonable time for response — typically 14 to 28 days. An unreasonably short deadline may not satisfy pre-action protocol requirements.

## Template 2: Cease and Desist Letter

### Purpose

A cease and desist letter demands that the recipient stop engaging in specified conduct that infringes the sender's rights. It is commonly used in intellectual property disputes (trademark infringement, copyright infringement, patent infringement), defamation matters, breaches of contract (particularly non-compete or non-solicitation clauses), and harassment situations.

### Template Text

---

**[FIRM LETTERHEAD]**

[DATE]

**BY [METHOD]**

[RECIPIENT NAME]
[RECIPIENT ADDRESS]

**Dear [RECIPIENT NAME],**

**Re: Cease and Desist — [BRIEF DESCRIPTION, e.g., "Infringement of [CLIENT NAME]'s Trademark"]**
**Our Ref: [FILE REFERENCE]**

We act for [CLIENT NAME] in relation to [DESCRIPTION OF RIGHTS, e.g., "the registered trademark '[TRADEMARK]' (Registration No. [NUMBER]) in Class [CLASS]"].

**Infringing Conduct**

It has come to our client's attention that you are [DESCRIPTION OF INFRINGING CONDUCT, e.g., "using the mark '[INFRINGING MARK]' in connection with [GOODS/SERVICES] on your website at [URL] and in marketing materials distributed in [LOCATION]"].

This conduct constitutes [LEGAL BASIS, e.g.]:
(a) infringement of our client's registered trademark contrary to [section 120 of the Trade Marks Act 1995 (Cth) / 15 USC s 1114 (Lanham Act) / section 10 of the Trade Marks Act 1994 (UK)];
(b) misleading or deceptive conduct in contravention of [section 18 of the Australian Consumer Law / section 43(a) of the Lanham Act]; and
(c) passing off at common law.

**Demand**

We demand that you immediately:

1. Cease all use of the mark '[INFRINGING MARK]' and any confusingly similar marks in connection with [GOODS/SERVICES];
2. Remove all infringing material from your website, social media accounts, marketing materials, signage, and packaging;
3. Deliver up or destroy all infringing goods and materials in your possession, custody, or control;
4. Provide a written undertaking, in the form enclosed, that you will not resume the infringing conduct; and
5. Provide a full account of [PROFITS EARNED / GOODS SOLD] using the infringing mark.

**Time for Compliance**

You must comply with demands 1 and 2 within [TIMEFRAME, e.g., "seven (7) days"] of the date of this letter. You must comply with demands 3, 4, and 5 within [TIMEFRAME, e.g., "twenty-one (21) days"] of the date of this letter.

**Consequences**

If you do not comply within the time specified, our client will commence proceedings seeking:
(a) injunctive relief restraining you from continuing the infringing conduct;
(b) damages or an account of profits;
(c) delivery up and destruction of infringing goods;
(d) costs, including on an indemnity basis; and
(e) any further relief the court considers appropriate.

Our client reserves all rights.

Yours faithfully,

[SOLICITOR NAME]
[FIRM NAME]

## Template 3: Engagement Letter (Retainer)

### Purpose

An engagement letter (also called a retainer letter or client agreement) establishes the terms of the solicitor-client relationship. It is a professional obligation in most jurisdictions to provide a written engagement letter at the commencement of the retainer. The letter defines the scope of work, the fee basis, the client's obligations, and the terms on which the retainer may be terminated.

### Professional Obligations

| Jurisdiction | Requirement | Source |
|-------------|-------------|--------|
| AU | Must provide a costs disclosure before or as soon as practicable after being retained | Legal Profession Uniform Law 2014 s 174 |
| US | Fee arrangement should be communicated to client, preferably in writing; contingency fees must be in writing | ABA Model Rule 1.5 |
| UK | Must provide best possible information about likely costs, including basis of charges | SRA Code of Conduct r 8.7 |
| CA (ON) | Must confirm scope of retainer in writing | Rules of Professional Conduct, r 3.2-1A |
| NZ | Must provide client with information about the basis on which fees will be charged | Lawyers and Conveyancers Act 2006 s 4; Client Care Rules |

### Template Text

---

**[FIRM LETTERHEAD]**

[DATE]

[CLIENT NAME]
[CLIENT ADDRESS]

**Dear [CLIENT NAME],**

**Re: Engagement Letter — [MATTER DESCRIPTION]**
**Our Ref: [FILE REFERENCE]**

Thank you for instructing [FIRM NAME] in relation to [MATTER DESCRIPTION]. We are pleased to confirm our engagement on the following terms.

**1. Scope of Work**

We are retained to [DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF SCOPE, e.g., "provide legal advice and representation in relation to your claim against [OPPOSING PARTY] arising from [DESCRIPTION OF DISPUTE]. Our retainer includes: reviewing the relevant documents, advising on your rights and options, drafting and filing a Statement of Claim, conducting discovery, preparing for and attending mediation, and (if necessary) representing you at trial."]

Our retainer does not include [EXCLUSIONS, e.g., "any appeal from the trial court's decision, any counterclaim brought by the opposing party, or any related taxation advice. If these matters arise, we will discuss them with you separately."]

**2. Supervising Solicitor**

The solicitor with primary responsibility for your matter is [SOLICITOR NAME], [TITLE]. Other solicitors, clerks, and paralegals may also work on your matter under [SOLICITOR NAME]'s supervision.

**3. Fees**

Our fees are calculated on the following basis:

[OPTION A — TIME-BASED:]
Fees are calculated on a time-spent basis at the following hourly rates (exclusive of GST):
- Partner: $[RATE] per hour
- Senior Associate: $[RATE] per hour
- Solicitor: $[RATE] per hour
- Paralegal: $[RATE] per hour

Time is recorded in six-minute units. These rates are reviewed annually and may be adjusted on reasonable notice.

[OPTION B — FIXED FEE:]
A fixed fee of $[AMOUNT] (exclusive of GST) for the scope of work described in clause 1. The fixed fee does not include [EXCLUSIONS].

[OPTION C — CONTINGENCY / CONDITIONAL FEE:]
[AU: A conditional costs agreement pursuant to [section 181 of the Legal Profession Uniform Law 2014]. If the matter is successful, our fees will be [PERCENTAGE]% of the amount recovered, or our time-based fees plus an uplift of [PERCENTAGE]% (not exceeding 25%), whichever is [GREATER/LESSER]. If the matter is unsuccessful, [NO FEE IS PAYABLE / the following fees are payable: [SPECIFY]].]

**4. Disbursements**

In addition to our fees, you are responsible for disbursements incurred in the conduct of the matter, including court filing fees, barrister's fees, expert fees, search fees, travel expenses, and photocopying. We will seek your approval before incurring any individual disbursement exceeding $[THRESHOLD].

**5. Billing and Payment**

We will render invoices [FREQUENCY, e.g., "monthly"]. Payment is due within [PAYMENT TERMS, e.g., "fourteen (14) days"] of the date of the invoice. We reserve the right to cease work if an invoice remains unpaid for more than [PERIOD].

**6. Estimates**

Based on the information currently available, we estimate that the total costs of this matter will be in the range of $[LOWER ESTIMATE] to $[UPPER ESTIMATE] (exclusive of GST and disbursements). This estimate is based on the assumptions set out in [SCHEDULE / BELOW] and may change if [CIRCUMSTANCES THAT MAY AFFECT ESTIMATE]. We will inform you promptly if it appears the estimate will be exceeded.

**7. Your Obligations**

You must:
(a) provide us with complete and accurate information and instructions promptly;
(b) provide us with all relevant documents;
(c) inform us of any changes in your contact details or circumstances relevant to the matter;
(d) pay our invoices within the time specified; and
(e) make yourself available for conferences, mediations, and court appearances as required.

**8. Confidentiality**

All communications between us are confidential and subject to legal professional privilege. We will not disclose your confidential information to any third party without your consent, except as required by law or as necessary for the conduct of the matter.

**9. Conflicts of Interest**

We have conducted a conflict check and are not aware of any conflict of interest that would prevent us from acting for you. If a conflict arises during the retainer, we will notify you immediately and take appropriate steps in accordance with our professional obligations.

**10. Termination**

You may terminate this retainer at any time by written notice to us. We may terminate this retainer on reasonable notice if [GROUNDS, e.g., "you fail to provide instructions or pay invoices, a conflict of interest arises that cannot be resolved, or we are required to withdraw in accordance with our professional obligations"].

Upon termination, you remain liable for fees and disbursements incurred up to the date of termination. We will provide your file to you or your new solicitor upon payment of all outstanding fees.

**11. Complaints**

If you are dissatisfied with our services, please raise the matter with [COMPLAINTS CONTACT]. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact [REGULATORY BODY, e.g., "the Legal Services Commissioner" / "the Solicitors Regulation Authority" / "your state bar association"].

**Acceptance**

Please sign and return the enclosed copy of this letter to confirm your instructions. If you have any questions, please do not hesitate to contact me.

Yours faithfully,

[SOLICITOR NAME]
[FIRM NAME]

---

**ACKNOWLEDGMENT**

I, [CLIENT NAME], confirm that I have read and understood this Engagement Letter and the attached Costs Disclosure, and I instruct [FIRM NAME] to act for me on the terms set out above.

Signature: ________________________
Name: [CLIENT NAME]
Date: [DATE]

## Template 4: Costs Agreement / Disclosure

See Section 3 of the Engagement Letter above. The costs disclosure is typically provided as an attachment to the engagement letter or as a separate document that satisfies the requirements of the Legal Profession Uniform Law 2014 s 174 (AU), ABA Model Rule 1.5 (US), or SRA Code r 8.7 (UK).

The key mandatory disclosure items (AU) are:

- The basis on which fees are calculated (time, fixed, conditional, or other)
- An estimate of total legal costs, or if an estimate cannot be given, the range of costs and the major variables
- The client's right to negotiate a costs agreement
- The client's right to receive an itemized bill
- The client's right to have costs assessed by a costs assessor
- The availability of costs dispute mechanisms
- Any uplift or premium that may be charged
- The approximate cost of any barrister or agent to be engaged

## Template 5: Without Prejudice Settlement Offer

### Purpose

A without prejudice settlement offer is a communication made in a genuine attempt to resolve a dispute. The "without prejudice" privilege protects such communications from being admitted in evidence if the negotiations fail and the matter proceeds to trial. The privilege is recognized at common law and by statute in all covered jurisdictions.

### Legal Effect

The without prejudice privilege applies when: (a) there is a genuine dispute between the parties; (b) the communication is made in a genuine attempt to resolve that dispute; and (c) the communication is expressly or impliedly made without prejudice. See *Field v Commissioner for Railways for NSW* (1957) 99 CLR 285.

A Calderbank offer (named after *Calderbank v Calderbank* [1975] 3 All ER 333) is a without prejudice offer that is "without prejudice save as to costs" — meaning it cannot be admitted on liability but can be admitted on the question of costs if the offeree fails to achieve a better result at trial than the offer.

### Template Text

---

**[FIRM LETTERHEAD]**

[DATE]

**WITHOUT PREJUDICE [SAVE AS TO COSTS]**

[OPPOSING SOLICITOR NAME]
[OPPOSING FIRM NAME]
[OPPOSING FIRM ADDRESS]

**Dear [NAME],**

**Re: [MATTER DESCRIPTION]**
**Our Ref: [FILE REFERENCE]**
**Your Ref: [OPPOSING REFERENCE]**

We are instructed by our client, [CLIENT NAME], to make the following offer of settlement on a without prejudice basis [save as to costs].

**Offer**

Our client offers to resolve this matter on the following terms:

1. [DEFENDANT/RESPONDENT] pays [PLAINTIFF/APPLICANT] the sum of $[AMOUNT] in full and final settlement of all claims between the parties in these proceedings.

2. [EACH PARTY BEARS ITS OWN COSTS / THE [PARTY] PAYS THE [OTHER PARTY]'S COSTS ON A [PARTY/PARTY / STANDARD] BASIS, AS AGREED OR ASSESSED.]

3. The parties execute a deed of release in terms to be agreed, recording that the settlement is in full and final satisfaction of all claims, demands, and causes of action between the parties arising from the subject matter of these proceedings.

4. [ADDITIONAL TERMS, e.g., "The terms of the settlement remain confidential."]

**Basis of Offer**

This offer is made having regard to [BRIEF STATEMENT OF THE MATTERS CONSIDERED, e.g., "the strength of our client's claim, the costs of further litigation, and the commercial desirability of resolving this matter promptly"]. It represents a genuine and reasonable attempt to resolve these proceedings.

**Time for Acceptance**

This offer remains open for acceptance for [TIMEFRAME, e.g., "twenty-eight (28) days"] from the date of this letter. After that date, the offer lapses unless extended in writing.

**Costs Consequences**

This offer is made on a Calderbank basis. If [OPPOSING PARTY] does not accept this offer and subsequently fails to achieve a more favorable result at trial, our client will rely on this letter in support of an application for indemnity costs from the date of this offer.

Yours faithfully,

[SOLICITOR NAME]
[FIRM NAME]

## Template 6: Notice of Termination of Retainer

### Purpose

A notice of termination of retainer formally ends the solicitor-client relationship. It may be issued by the client (who has an absolute right to terminate at any time) or by the solicitor (who may only terminate on proper grounds and with reasonable notice to avoid prejudicing the client). Professional obligations require the solicitor to give reasonable notice, return the client's documents, and not terminate at a time that would materially prejudice the client.

### Template Text

---

**[FIRM LETTERHEAD]**

[DATE]

[CLIENT NAME]
[CLIENT ADDRESS]

**Dear [CLIENT NAME],**

**Re: Termination of Retainer — [MATTER DESCRIPTION]**
**Our Ref: [FILE REFERENCE]**

We write to advise that [FIRM NAME] is terminating our retainer to act for you in the above matter, effective [DATE — must provide reasonable notice].

**Reason for Termination**

[SELECT APPROPRIATE REASON — note that in some circumstances the solicitor cannot disclose the specific reason:]

(a) [We have not received instructions from you despite our requests dated [DATES].]
(b) [Our invoices dated [DATES] totaling $[AMOUNT] remain outstanding despite our reminders dated [DATES].]
(c) [A conflict of interest has arisen that prevents us from continuing to act.]
(d) [We are no longer able to act in this matter for professional reasons that we are not at liberty to disclose. / There has been a breakdown in the solicitor-client relationship.]

**Your File**

Upon termination, you are entitled to your file. We will release your file to you or your new solicitor upon:
(a) your written direction specifying where the file should be sent; and
(b) [payment of our outstanding fees and disbursements / subject to our right to exercise a lien over the file for unpaid fees, which we will exercise/waive as follows: [SPECIFY]].

[NOTE: The solicitor's lien over the client's file for unpaid fees is recognized at common law but is subject to the court's discretion. In some jurisdictions and circumstances, the court may order release of the file notwithstanding the lien, particularly where the client's interests would be prejudiced.]

**Upcoming Deadlines**

We draw your attention to the following matters that require action:

1. [DEADLINE 1, e.g., "Your defense is due to be filed by [DATE]."]
2. [DEADLINE 2, e.g., "A mediation is scheduled for [DATE]."]
3. [DEADLINE 3, e.g., "The limitation period for your claim expires on [DATE]."]

It is essential that you engage new solicitors promptly to protect your interests. If you do not engage new solicitors and take the necessary steps, your rights may be prejudiced.

**Outstanding Fees**

Our final invoice in the amount of $[AMOUNT] is enclosed. Payment is due within [PAYMENT TERMS] of the date of this letter.

We wish you well in the continued conduct of this matter.

Yours faithfully,

[SOLICITOR NAME]
[FIRM NAME]

## Common Pitfalls Across All Correspondence Templates

1. **Sending privileged correspondence to the wrong recipient.** Without prejudice correspondence should only be sent to the opposing party's legal representatives, not to the court (unless a Calderbank costs argument is being made after judgment).

2. **Failing to mark correspondence correctly.** If a letter is intended to be without prejudice, it should be marked as such. While the privilege depends on substance not form, clear marking avoids disputes about whether the privilege applies.

3. **Threatening proceedings without authority.** A solicitor must have instructions from the client before threatening legal proceedings. Threatening proceedings without instructions may breach conduct rules.

4. **Terminating retainer at a prejudicial time.** A solicitor who terminates a retainer on the eve of trial or immediately before a limitation period expires may face professional conduct complaints and liability for the client's loss.

5. **Failing to include mandatory costs disclosure.** In Australia, failure to provide costs disclosure in accordance with the Legal Profession Uniform Law may result in the solicitor being unable to recover fees and may trigger professional conduct consequences.
