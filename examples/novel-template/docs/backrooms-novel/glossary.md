# Glossary

**no-clip** — The involuntary act of passing through solid matter and emerging in the Backrooms. No reliable mechanism has been identified. It appears to occur most frequently at moments of severe inattention, extreme fatigue, or acute perceptual distraction. There is no known method of triggering a no-clip intentionally.

**Level** — A discrete architectural region of the Backrooms with consistent geometry, consistent environmental conditions, and consistent hazard profile. Level boundaries are not always visible; passage between Levels often occurs without warning. The total number of Levels is unknown and may be infinite.

**Entity** — A non-human organism that inhabits the Backrooms. Entities vary enormously in appearance, behavior, and apparent intelligence. Most are hostile; a small number are indifferent or, in rare documented cases, cooperative. Entity taxonomy is contested — different expeditions have produced incompatible classification systems.

**clipping out** — The reverse of no-clipping: returning to baseline reality from the Backrooms. Clipping out is far rarer than no-clipping in and far less well understood. Several exit methods have been documented but none is reproducible on demand.

**almond water** — A liquid found in vending machines and standing pools throughout certain Levels. It has a faint almond odor and a mild, indeterminate flavor. Despite its ambiguous origin, it appears to be safe to drink and provides genuine sustenance. It is the primary survival resource for extended Backrooms expeditions.

**sanity** — In Backrooms survivor accounts, a measurable psychological resource that degrades under exposure to entities, environmental anomalies, and extended isolation. When a wanderer's sanity is sufficiently depleted, perceptual distortions begin, judgment fails, and the risk of fatal decision-making increases substantially. The relationship between Backrooms-induced psychological degradation and conventional psychiatric diagnoses is not established.
