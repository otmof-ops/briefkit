## 13.4 — Crankshaft and Main Bearings — Installing

1. Wipe crank main journals with lint-free cloth and acetone.
2. Install upper main bearing shells in the block saddles, aligning locating tangs.
3. Lay the crankshaft into the block dry, no oil.
4. Set Plastigauge across each main journal perpendicular to the crank axis.
5. Install lower main bearing shells in the main caps.
6. Torque main bolts to 65 Nm + 90 degrees without rotating the crank.
7. Remove caps and read clearance from the Plastigauge calibration card.
8. Target main bearing clearance: 0.018 to 0.038 mm.
9. Remove the crankshaft and clean Plastigauge residue.
10. Apply assembly lube to upper and lower bearing shells.
11. Reinstall the crankshaft.
12. Reinstall main caps and torque to final spec.
13. Verify the crankshaft spins freely by hand.
14. Measure crankshaft endplay with a dial indicator at the end of the crank.

## 13.5 — Connecting Rods and Pistons — Installing

1. Verify all ring gaps are within specified range with the ring squarely in the bore.
2. Install rings on pistons with a ring expander, observing top markings.
3. Stagger ring end gaps at 120 degrees, avoiding the thrust face.
4. Orient piston with arrow toward the timing belt end.
5. Orient rod with bearing oil squirter toward the exhaust side.
6. Install wrist pin and circlips, verifying full seating.
7. Install upper rod bearing shell in the rod big end.
8. Lubricate bearing surface with assembly lube.
9. Install piston in ring compressor and clamp.
10. Lower piston and rod assembly into the cylinder from above.
11. Tap the piston in with the wooden handle of a hammer.
12. Guide the rod onto the crank journal as the piston enters the bore.
13. Install rod cap with lubed bearing in original orientation.
14. Torque ARP2000 rod bolts using stretch gauge to 0.0063 inch.
15. Verify the rod moves freely without binding.
16. Measure rod side clearance with a feeler gauge.

## 13.6 — Critical Notes

**WARNING** — Oil squirter alignment must be verified visually before first start. Squirters must aim at the underside of the piston crown, not the cylinder wall.

**WARNING** — Rod bolt stretch measurement is non-negotiable for ARP2000. Torque-only installations have a documented failure history at high cylinder pressure.

**WARNING** — Never reuse single-use stretch bolts. Head bolts, main bolts, rod bolts, crank pulley bolt, and flywheel bolts are all single use.

**NOTE** — Bore taper and out-of-round tolerances from the piston manufacturer are stricter than OEM specification. Always follow the manufacturer specification.
